### Sanity check — is this "too cheap to be true," or correctly cheap?

This is exactly the Howard Marks "second-level thinking" question the task asks to weigh explicitly:

- **The bear case is real, not noise.** FY2025 net income fell ~12% (different basis) to ~−11.8% on a per-ADS diluted-EPS basis; Q1 2026 net income fell a further **17% YoY**, missing consensus EPS by **43%**; and the FY2026 consensus EPS estimate was cut **−17.8%** (RMB77.13→RMB63.39) post-Q1, itself implying **negative** YoY EPS growth for a second consecutive year. Temu's international expansion faces **escalating, real tariff/regulatory risk** (US/EU de-minimis exemption changes specifically target the low-value China-direct parcel model Temu depends on), and competition from Shein/Amazon/TikTok Shop/Alibaba/JD is intensifying on every front. The market dropping this stock ~14-19% over 1-3 months on a real earnings miss and a guided-down outlook is not an irrational mispricing in isolation.
- **But the magnitude of the re-rating looks disproportionate to the magnitude of the fundamental deterioration.** A double-digit percentage decline in near-term earnings does not, on its own, justify an EV/EBIT of 3.87× for a business that is (a) still growing revenue at double digits (+10-11% YoY even in the "miss" quarters), (b) still GAAP-profitable with a >20% net margin, (c) sitting on a **$61.7B net-cash position that is itself larger than the entire $54.1B enterprise value** — meaning the market is valuing PDD's *entire operating business* (ex-cash) at roughly **negative $7.6B** ($54.12B EV − implied... actually EV already nets out cash, so EV $54.1B IS the operating-business valuation, against $14B of GAAP EBIT — i.e. a ~3.9x multiple on a business generating >20% net margins and still growing). For comparison, even a structurally-declining, ex-growth retailer would typically command a higher EV/EBIT than 3.87× absent genuine going-concern or expropriation risk.
- **The most defensible "real headwind priced in" interpretation** is that the market is pricing in (a) a multi-year margin trough as Temu's international subsidies and the new first-party-brand initiative (RMB100B over 3 years) continue to suppress near-term profitability, PLUS (b) a meaningful "China-ADR discount" (delisting/geopolitical risk premium that applies to PDD regardless of operating performance — the same discount embedded in Alibaba, JD, and other US-listed China names). Both are legitimate, durable-ish discounts — but neither plausibly justifies pricing the *entire operating business* at under 4x EBIT for a company growing revenue double-digits with >20% net margins.

**Conclusion: the score is not a false signal from a stale/erroneous price — it reflects a genuine, large valuation gap, even after fully crediting the bear case.** The recommendation below sizes for this by using a wide margin of safety on the fair-value side (see §8) rather than dismissing the framework's output.
