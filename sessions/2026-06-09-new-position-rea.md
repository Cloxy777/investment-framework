## Business Overview

REA Group is Australia's dominant online real estate marketplace, operating realestate.com.au alongside commercial/developer and financial services platforms. News Corp holds 61% of the register. The platform commands approximately 10× more consumer visits than its nearest competitor (Domain), giving it a winner-take-most network effect: agents pay for listings because buyers are there; buyers come because all the listings are there. Switching costs and brand recognition are extremely high.

**Additional verticals:**
- **REA India (Elara Technologies):** Growing but still loss-making online property portal. Results flow through P&L as equity-accounted losses (~A$127M difference between FY2025 EBIT A$922M and NPAT A$564M, net of taxes and equity losses).
- **PropTrack:** AI-powered property data/analytics platform (fully owned).
- **Financial Services:** Mortgage brokerage and data products.

## Qualitative Pass — 5 Questions

**1. Why are margins high?**
Structural: REA operates a marketplace with near-zero marginal cost per additional listing. Revenue is primarily subscription-style fees from real estate agents (Premiere+, Platinum products) with 65%+ gross margins. Market dominance (10× traffic advantage) gives REA monopoly-like pricing power in Australia — agents have no credible alternative that reaches equivalent buyer audiences. There is no "lucky cycle" element: margins have been stable/expanding over a decade through multiple property market cycles.

**2. What would it take to compete?**
Domain (ASX: DHG) has been trying for 15+ years and remains at ~1/10th the traffic. To displace REA, a new entrant would need to simultaneously attract enough buyers to entice agents AND attract enough listings to attract buyers — a classic cold-start network problem that is essentially impossible at this stage. News Corp's 61% ownership provides additional financial and distribution resources. The switching cost for agents is psychological and economic: listing elsewhere risks reduced exposure to the deepest buyer pool.

**3. Management capital allocation (5–10 years)?**
Australian core: exceptional capital efficiency (ROIC 34%, low capex). International bets: REA India (Elara Technologies) is the main capital sink — strategic long-term optionality but current drag on earnings. Share buybacks and growing dividends in core Australia. Key risk: continued capital export to India without clear path to profitability timeline is a monitoring flag. FY2025 was the first year without a CEO (CEO retirement in H1 FY2025, CEO search ongoing per search results) — leadership continuity to watch.

**4. Growth next 3–5 years?**
- **Depth product penetration:** Moving agents from basic to Premiere/Platinum listings (currently ~50% penetration; can grow toward 70-80%) — adds revenue without volume growth
- **Financial services:** Mortgage origination/brokerage cross-sell via the realestate.com.au funnel; market share still tiny
- **REA India:** Large TAM (~A$12B addressable), growing fast, but execution and regulatory risk are high
- **PropTrack data/analytics:** B2B licensing of AI-powered property valuation/data
- FY2026E revenue growth forecast: ~12-15% on a higher base; H1 FY2026 showed 5% revenue growth (slower due to H1 FY2025 being a particularly strong comp), but full year expected to recover

**5. Bear case?**
- Property market severe correction → listing volumes contract 20-30% → revenue down despite higher yield per listing
- Domain or a new entrant (backed by a major tech co — Alphabet, Meta, or a Chinese platform) credibly replicates the consumer experience → network effect erodes over 3-5 years
- REA India capital requirements dramatically exceed current expectations, creating dilutive capital raises or crowding out Australian shareholder returns
- CEO vacancy extends, creating management uncertainty

**6. Disruption vector?**
AI-powered property search/recommendation could in theory reduce the role of aggregator platforms (buyers go direct to AI agents, not portals). However: (1) REA is investing heavily in AI via PropTrack; (2) listing data is REA's moat, not just the search interface; (3) agents retain the key relationships with buyers and sellers. Low disruption risk over 5-year horizon; watch over a 10-year horizon.
