**Determination: ROE ~20% is treated as the primary, reliable signal — PASS.** Rationale:
- The ~20% ROE reading is corroborated by **4 independent sources** within a tight 1-percentage-point band, versus a single lower ROIC reading from one source (GuruFocus) that itself shows internal inconsistency (11.67% vs 16.47% across its own pages).
- Tencent's investment portfolio sits on the asset side of the balance sheet (equity method / FVTPL investments) and is funded partly by retained earnings — it does inflate the *equity* base somewhat (similar in spirit to PDD's cash-hoard distortion, but in the opposite direction for ROIC vs ROE: a large investment-asset base depresses ROIC by inflating invested capital, while a strong, profitable core business still drives a healthy ROE off that larger equity base). A ~20% ROE on a base that includes ~RMB1.2T of total equity (including the investment portfolio) is, if anything, a **conservative** read of the core operating business's true returns — excluding the investment portfolio from the equity base would likely push the "core operator" ROE higher still.
- This is the same logic the framework's own guidance anticipates ("flag if equity-heavy structure makes ROE unreliable as a ROIC stand-in") — but here it cuts in Tencent's *favor*: the distortion likely understates rather than overstates true operating returns.

## 6. Phase 02 — Full Score Calculation

**FCF Yield — 40% weight**

```
FCF Yield = FCF / Market Cap = HKD 211.59B / HKD 4,181.7B = 5.058%

FCF_Score = clamp(100 × (1 − FCF_Yield% / 10), 0, 100)
          = clamp(100 × (1 − 5.058/10), 0, 100)
          = clamp(100 × 0.4942, 0, 100)

```
→ Contribution: 49.4 × 0.40 = **19.76**

**EV/EBIT — 40% weight (PEG not applicable, redistributed from 25%)**

```
Market Cap = 9.02B shares × HKD 463.60 = HKD 4,181.7B
Net Cash   = HKD 124.10B (RMB107.1B × 1.158688)
EV         = Market Cap − Net Cash = HKD 4,181.7B − HKD 124.10B = HKD 4,057.6B

EV/EBIT (GAAP) = HKD 4,057.6B / HKD 279.93B (FY2025 GAAP operating profit)

```
→ Contribution: 10.9 × 0.40 = **4.36**

*(Sensitivity: using non-IFRS operating profit (HKD 325.18B) instead gives EV/EBIT = 12.48×, EV/EBIT_Score = 2.1 — slightly cheaper, doesn't change the overall conclusion. GAAP EBIT used as primary per Rule 6 normalization preference and consistency with the PDD session's approach.)*

**Forward PE (PRIMARY formula — 10yr low/high range available) — 20% weight**

```
Forward PE  = 13.21×
10yr Low PE  = 6.26×
10yr High PE = 42.19×

FwdPE_Score (raw) = clamp((Forward PE − 10yr Low PE) / (10yr High PE − 10yr Low PE) × 100, 0, 100)
                   = clamp((13.21 − 6.26) / (42.19 − 6.26) × 100, 0, 100)
                   = clamp(6.95 / 35.93 × 100, 0, 100)
                   = clamp(19.34, 0, 100)
                   = 19.3
```

**Historical PE Modifier (Upgrade 2, additive to the FwdPE_Score sub-score — applies because the PRIMARY range formula was used)**

Per valuation-scoring.md: *"Position the company's forward PE within its own trailing 10-year PE range, then apply the Historical PE Modifier (Upgrade 2)... additive: >20% below 10yr avg PE → −10..."* — the modifier adjusts the **FwdPE_Score sub-score itself**, before that sub-score is weighted at 20%.

```
Deviation from 10yr avg = (Forward PE − 10yr Avg PE) / 10yr Avg PE
                         = (13.21 − 25.73) / 25.73
                         = −48.7%
```
−48.7% is **>20% below** the 10-year average → modifier = **−10**.

*Structural Quality Override check (Upgrade 2 addendum):* the override exists to **waive a penalty for being expensive** when expensiveness reflects genuine structural improvement — it does not apply here, since Tencent is *cheap* relative to history, not expensive. If anything, the override's logic reinforces the cheap signal: FY2025 showed genuine structural improvement (operating profit +16-18%, ROE ~20%, net cash +40%) *despite* the multiple sitting 48.7% below its 10-year average — i.e., the business has gotten better while the multiple has compressed, the inverse of "multiple expansion without underlying improvement" that the override is designed to catch. The **−10 modifier stands** (and is, if anything, conservative).

```
FwdPE_Score (adjusted) = clamp(FwdPE_Score(raw) − 10, 0, 100)
                        = clamp(19.34 − 10, 0, 100)
                        = 9.3
```
→ Contribution: 9.3 × 0.20 = **1.86**

### Sanity check — why is a business with improving fundamentals (ROE ~20%, operating profit +16-18%, net cash +40%) trading 48.7% below its own historical average multiple?

This is the Howard Marks "second-level thinking" question:

- **The 10-year average (25.73×) is heavily influenced by 2018-2021**, an era of much higher growth expectations and a pre-regulatory-crackdown multiple regime for Chinese internet platforms. The 2022 trough (6.26×) reflects the regulatory crackdown itself. **Today's 13.21× sits closer to the trough than the peak**, despite FY2025 showing the business is now *past* the crackdown-era trough operationally (profits and margins recovering, net cash building, buybacks ongoing).
- **The gap is best explained by a persistent "China-ADR / China-tech discount"** — geopolitical risk (US-China tensions, delisting overhang for US-listed China names, regulatory unpredictability) appears to be keeping the multiple compressed *independent of* the operating recovery, similar to the discount flagged in the PDD session (though PDD's situation also has company-specific earnings deterioration; Tencent's does not — its earnings are *growing*).
- **This looks like a genuine, durable valuation gap rather than a modelling artifact**: every sub-score component (FCF yield ~5%, EV/EBIT ~14.5×, Forward PE deep in the bottom quintile of its own 10-year range) independently points toward "cheap," and the qualitative case (WeChat super-app moat — one of the strongest screened to date per the EM session) supports the quality side of "quality value."
