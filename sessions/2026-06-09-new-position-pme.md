## 8. Qualitative Assessment Summary

(Full qualitative analysis completed in APAC-EX-JP screening session — see `sessions/2026-06-09-screening-apac-ex-jp.md`. Key points below.)

1. **Why are margins high?** Visage 7 is a per-study-fee SaaS with near-zero marginal delivery cost. Hospital radiology departments can't easily switch (radiologist re-training is 6–12 months; deep EHR/PACS integrations must be re-certified). Once PME wins a system, annuity revenue is highly durable.

2. **Moat:** Near-monopoly in the performance tier of radiology SaaS. Winning the University of Colorado ($170M, 10-yr) and scale of US academic medical centre contracts demonstrates the moat is deepening, not eroding. No competitor (Philips, Sectra, Fujifilm) has matched Visage 7 on large-dataset 3D streaming at comparable performance.

3. **Capital allocation:** Founder-operator (Sam Hupert, ~23% stake). No debt. No M&A history. Cash accumulates then is returned via dividends (28% dividend increase in H1 FY2026). Exemplary.

4. **Growth sources 3–5 years:** US academic medical centre penetration (each win = 10-year recurring contract), European expansion, AI integration (Annalise/Microsoft partnerships adding per-study value), cardiology modules (Ohio State specifically renewed to adopt cardiology module = expanded ARPU).

5. **Bear case:** Lower-than-expected renewal ARPU per study; intensifying competition from AI-native entrants (Rad AI, Aidoc) as AI becomes table-stakes; hospital sector consolidation reducing PME's pricing leverage; Hupert succession risk.

6. **Disruption vector:** AI-native radiology platforms (e.g., from Nuance/Microsoft or Google Health) could commoditize the viewer and shift value to AI diagnostic layers. Long-run risk, not 5-year risk. PME's AI partnerships partially hedge this.

**Qualitative verdict: exceptional business. No moat concerns. The issue is purely valuation — the market has already priced in the quality.**
