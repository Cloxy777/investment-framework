# 2026-06-07 — Framework fixes: investor-philosophy alignment audit

**What changed:**

A philosophy-alignment audit checked the framework's rules against the investors it already cites as its evidence base — Buffett, Munger, Lynch, Greenblatt, Marks (see [strategy.md](../framework/strategy.md)'s Hybrid Upgrades intro: "evidence-based upgrades from a 40-year cross-framework stress test vs Buffett · Munger · Lynch · Fisher · Marks"). It surfaced four findings. All four are now resolved:

1. **New reference doc — [framework/investor-philosophy-alignment.md](../framework/investor-philosophy-alignment.md):** a high-level summary of each investor's core tenets, plus a 5-point "alignment check" (price-action test, turnover test, mean-reversion test, macro-veto test, concentration test) to run before adopting or changing any future rule. Linked from [strategy.md](../framework/strategy.md) (Hybrid Upgrades intro) and [CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md) (orientation), so it surfaces automatically whenever either is read before a framework change.

2. **Phase 05 Dynamic Trimming — trim trigger raised from Score 6–7 to Score 8+** ([strategy.md](../framework/strategy.md)). Previously the framework began trimming a position the moment it crossed from "Cheap" into merely "Fair Value" — a materially higher-turnover posture than Buffett ("our favorite holding period is forever"), Munger ("sit on your ass investing" / the "croupier's take" on trading friction), or Terry Smith (~3–4% annual turnover, "do nothing") actually practice. Score 6–7 is now hold-and-watch only — it already carried a "Watchlist only, no new entry" label in Phase 03, so there was no reason for it to *also* be a trim trigger. Bands shift up one tier: Score 8 → trim 25–30%, Score 9 → trim to 50%, Score 10 → trim to 1–2% tracking; "Score 10 sustained 2+ quarters → full exit" (Phase 06) is unchanged. The framework keeps its teeth at the extremes, where even these long-term compounders do trim (e.g., Buffett's 2024 Apple cut once that position became dramatically oversized and rich).
   *(Note: this directly revisits the trim mechanism that [decisions/2026-06-07-framework-change-position-cap.md](2026-06-07-framework-change-position-cap.md) explicitly chose to leave untouched earlier the same day, after an even-earlier draft proposed changing it. It was reopened here only after surfacing that exact conflict to the user directly and receiving an explicit instruction — "raise the trigger to Score 8+" — in this session. Recorded so a future reviewer sees this was a conscious re-decision, not an inconsistency.)*

3. **Rate Environment Gate Step 1 — converted from a hard "do not open" veto to an additive scoring modifier** ([strategy.md](../framework/strategy.md)). Spread ≥ +1.5% → no change; Spread < +1.5% → **+0.5 to the valuation score** (a yellow flag that raises the bar, rather than an outright block). Buffett treats interest rates as "gravity" on asset values — a valuation *anchor*, not a binary switch — and Lynch ("thirteen minutes a year on the economy") and Munger both warn against letting a macro read *override* a strong bottom-up case. This keeps the rate-sensitivity (the bar still gets harder to clear) without letting it veto an exceptional company outright, and now mirrors how Step 2's Rate Regime Modifier already works.
   *(Note: the NVDA rescore session — [sessions/2026-06-07-rescore-nvda.md](../sessions/2026-06-07-rescore-nvda.md) — used this gate in its old hard-block form to confirm "do not add here." Re-running that check under the new modifier wouldn't flip the underlying conclusion in NVDA's case — the position was already a trim candidate on valuation grounds — but any future session should apply the new modifier form, not the retired hard gate.)*

4. **Upgrade 2 (Historical PE Relative Modifier) — added a "Structural Quality Override"** ([strategy.md](../framework/strategy.md)). The "+1 expensive" adjustment no longer applies when the multiple expansion is accompanied by a genuine *structural* improvement — margin expansion, ROIC improvement, or qualifying growth-CapEx reinvestment per Upgrade 1. This reflects Howard Marks' "second-level thinking" (a historical average is only a useful anchor if the business hasn't structurally changed — "what's different this time?") and closes a quiet internal contradiction: Upgrade 1 already concedes that raw multiples *understate* quality for moat-building reinvestment (the MSFT Owner Earnings example), so without this override, Upgrade 2 would mechanically penalize the very reinvestment Upgrade 1 credits.

5. **Upgrade 6 (Momentum Filter) — retired, not replaced** ([strategy.md](../framework/strategy.md), [operating-brief.md](../framework/operating-brief.md)). The original rule gated new-position entries on whether price sat above or below the 200-day moving average — a price-action/technical signal that (a) directly contradicted this framework's own non-negotiable Rule 0, *"act only on documented triggers — a valuation-score change or a fundamental event, never on price movement alone"* ([CLAUDE.md](../CLAUDE.md)), and (b) sits squarely against Buffett/Munger/Graham's lifelong, explicit rejection of chart-based decision triggers ("technical analysis is a bunch of nonsense" — Munger). Its protective intent — don't buy into a deteriorating situation — is already served by Phase 04's Quality Watch, Narrative Check, and Short Thesis Engagement, all fundamentals-based, so no replacement mechanism was invented. The heading and number are kept (rather than renumbered) so references to Upgrade 7 — and any future ones — stay stable.

**Why:**

The user asked for a precise review of whether the framework's own rules could be seen as controversial against the philosophies of the investors it *already cites* as its evidence base — specifically so that future framework edits can be checked for alignment without re-deriving the analysis each time. This audit ran that review, found four points of friction (one purely additive — the reference doc — and three rule changes), and resolved the two that touched live, contested mechanisms (the trim trigger and the rate gate) only after putting the conflicts to the user directly, given that the trim trigger in particular had been the subject of a same-day revert just hours earlier.

**Follow-up:**
- Any holding currently sitting at Score 6–7 that was trimmed under the *old* 6–7 trigger should be reviewed at the next `/rebalance` — under the new rule, a merely-fairly-valued position is a hold, not a trim, so a recent trim executed at that band may now look premature in hindsight (not wrong under the rule that existed at the time, but worth knowing the rule has since moved).
- The next `/rescore` or `/rebalance` session that touches the Rate Environment Gate or Upgrade 2 should apply the new modifier/override forms — not the retired hard-gate or unconditional-penalty forms — and can use [investor-philosophy-alignment.md](../framework/investor-philosophy-alignment.md) as the standing reference for *why*.
