# Glossary

Standing reference of financial jargon, abbreviations, and framework-specific terms used across this repo. User isn't a finance professional, so every term that could plausibly be unfamiliar gets a plain-English definition here.

**How this is used:** per [operating-brief.md](operating-brief.md), every analysis response, session log, decision log, and PR description must end with a "Glossary" section listing the terms from *this* file that actually appear in that output (not the whole file). If a response uses a term not here yet, add it to this file in the same session and then cite it — this file is expected to grow over time.

Alphabetical within each group.

---

## General financial & valuation terms

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **21 CFR Part 11** | The FDA regulation governing electronic records and electronic signatures in regulated (e.g. pharmaceutical/clinical) workflows. Compliance with it is a significant source of customer switching costs for regulated-industry software vendors like Veeva — replacing a Part-11-validated system means re-validating the replacement, retraining staff, and risking a compliance gap in the interim. |
| **1-SD (one standard deviation) move** | The price range a stock is expected to stay within roughly 68% of the time, derived from options-implied volatility over a given time window — a market-priced probability band, not a prediction. |
| **10-K (Annual Report)** | The annual financial-disclosure report a US public company must file with the SEC, containing full audited financial statements, MD&A (management's discussion and analysis), and risk factors — the primary Rule-0-compliant fundamentals source this framework uses for SEC-registered filers (e.g. AAPL, OUST) when a bulk data vendor is unavailable. |
| **10-Q (Quarterly Report)** | The quarterly financial-disclosure report a US public company files with the SEC between annual 10-Ks, containing unaudited (reviewed) financial statements for the most recent fiscal quarter — used in this framework to reconstruct trailing-twelve-month (TTM) figures between annual reports, as in the 2026-07-06 AAPL session. |
| **10b5-1 Trading Plan** | A pre-arranged SEC Rule 10b5-1 stock trading plan that lets a company insider (executive, director) schedule stock sales/purchases on a fixed schedule or formula set up in advance, as an affirmative defense against insider-trading allegations, since the trades were arranged before any access to non-public information about the specific triggering event. A sale executed under a pre-existing plan (e.g. Chevron CFO Eimear Bonner's Jan 2026 option-exercise-and-sale) is scheduled, process-driven selling, not a discretionary signal about the insider's view of the stock — this framework does not treat it as satisfying the Turnaround Sub-Gate's insider-buying condition, which requires actual discretionary open-market buying. |
| **340B Drug Pricing Program** | A US federal program requiring drug manufacturers to sell outpatient drugs at a discount to certain hospitals/clinics serving low-income patients, in exchange for Medicare/Medicaid coverage. Manufacturers hold balance-sheet provisions (reserves) against expected 340B rebate obligations; a **reversal** of part of that provision (e.g. following a revised estimate of what's actually owed) is a one-off, **non-cash** accounting gain that boosts reported operating profit and net income in the period recognized without reflecting any underlying improvement in the business — Novo Nordisk's Q1 2026 results included a DKK 26.8B (~$4.2B) 340B provision reversal that inflated reported operating profit growth (+65% CER) far above the much weaker adjusted, ex-reversal figure (−6% CER), a case this framework normalizes out per Rule 6 ("normalize before you value"). |
| **424B4 (Prospectus)** | The final prospectus a company files with the SEC after its S-1 registration statement is declared effective, disclosing the final IPO offer price and terms — SpaceX (SPCX) filed its 424B4 on 12 June 2026, the day its IPO priced, cited in this framework's 2026-07-13 SPCX session. |
| **52-week range** | The lowest and highest price a stock has traded at over the past year — a quick gauge of where the current price sits within its recent trading history. |
| **ABL Credit Facility (Asset-Based Lending)** | A revolving credit line sized against (and collateralized by) a company's own current assets (typically inventory and receivables) rather than its cash flow or general creditworthiness — Lucid Group's (LCID) five-year, $1.0B ABL Credit Facility (entered 2022) is cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 LCID session as one of the company's several credit facilities layered on top of its equity/preferred-stock capital raises. *(New term.)* |
| **ACV (Annual Contract Value)** | The annualized value of a signed customer contract at the time of signing — a forward-looking bookings/sales metric, distinct from ARR (which measures currently-recognized recurring revenue run-rate). Companies report incremental ACV for a specific fast-growing product line (e.g. ServiceNow's AI product suite, "Now Assist," tracking toward a raised $1.5B 2026 ACV target) as an early adoption signal ahead of that revenue showing up in reported subscription revenue/ARR. |
| **Adjusted EBITDA** | A company's own non-GAAP variant of EBITDA that further strips out items management deems non-recurring or non-operational (e.g. restructuring charges, stock-based compensation, separation/transaction costs) — not directly comparable to a GAAP-derived EBITDA (EBIT + D&A) computed from filed financials without those add-backs. This framework's own EBITDA figures are GAAP-derived unless explicitly noted as "adjusted"; mixing the two without flagging the difference understates the mismatch (see the Mobility Global note in this framework's 2026-07-05 SPGI session, where Mobility's own disclosed *adjusted* EBITDA was netted against a GAAP-derived consolidated EBITDA as an approximation). |
| **Adjusted Free Cash Flow** | MercadoLibre's own non-GAAP FCF variant: operating cash flow less capex, further adjusted to strip out the swings in fintech customer-fund cash/investments, loans receivable, and fintech-related loans payable that the company itself treats as "working capital of the Fintech activities" rather than core operating cash flow. Materially smaller (and occasionally negative quarter-to-quarter) than a naive GAAP OCF−CapEx figure, which is inflated by Mercado Pago's credit-portfolio funding dynamics — used as the primary FCF basis in MELI's Quality Score addendum (2026-07-10) specifically because the GAAP figure would overstate cash-earnings quality; the GAAP-basis figure is still shown as a cross-check, per this framework's "show every calculation" discipline. |
| **AOI (Adjusted Operating Income)** | Stellantis's own non-GAAP operating-profit measure, which excludes one-off items (restructuring, EV product-plan write-downs, warranty provisions) that are included in its GAAP/IFRS operating result — same "company's own non-recurring-items-stripped-out metric" caveat as Adjusted EBITDA above. Stellantis's FY2025 AOI was −€842M (−0.5% margin) vs. a GAAP/IFRS operating loss of −€26.25B for the same year — this framework scores off the filed IFRS figure, not the non-GAAP AOI, per the "guidance/self-reported metrics are not scored" principle. |
| **ADR (American Depositary Receipt)** | A US-exchange-listed security representing shares of a non-US company, letting US investors trade it in USD without using a foreign exchange directly. ADRs can represent a 1:1, multiple, or fractional ratio of home-market shares — for Nokia's NOK, this framework confirmed (rather than assumed) a 1-for-1 ratio against the Helsinki-listed ordinary share by cross-checking IBKR's live USD quote against Yahoo Finance's EUR-denominated financials converted at the prevailing spot FX rate; the two were internally consistent, no ratio adjustment needed. |
| **ADS (American Depositary Share)** | The individual underlying share represented by an ADR (the ADR is technically the negotiable certificate; "ADS" and "ADR" are used near-interchangeably in practice). Carnival plc's ADSs were cancelled and exchanged 1-for-1 into Carnival Corporation Ltd. common shares as part of the May 2026 dual-listed-company unification. |
| **ADR premium (parity gap)** | The percentage by which an ADR's US-dollar price diverges from the value implied by its underlying home-market ordinary shares at the prevailing FX rate and ADR ratio — theoretically kept near zero by arbitrage (buying the cheaper leg, selling the richer one, converting between the two), but able to widen sharply when arbitrage/conversion is constrained (e.g. a very recently listed ADR, thin two-way conversion volume, or a trading halt on one leg). SK Hynix's ADR (SKHY) traded at premiums as high as ~51% over its Korea Exchange-listed ordinary shares (000660) in its first week of Nasdaq trading (10–16 July 2026), before compressing sharply after a 15.4%, single-day, circuit-breaker-triggering plunge in the Seoul-listed shares on 13 July 2026 — a live, material price-integrity risk flagged in this framework's 2026-07-17 SKHY session, since a US live-price fetch (Rule 0) can diverge substantially from the economically "true" value of the underlying business during this kind of dislocation. *(New term.)* |
| **Sponsored ADR / Unsponsored ADR** | An **unsponsored** ADR is created unilaterally by a depositary bank without the underlying company's involvement or cooperation — typically thinly traded, priced inconsistently against the home-market shares, and carrying no company-provided periodic SEC reporting. A **sponsored** ADR is established with the company's direct participation (a formal deposit agreement, its own SEC registration, and ongoing periodic filings — 20-F/6-K for a foreign private issuer). SK Hynix's ADR (SKHY) upgraded from a thinly-traded unsponsored OTC program (ticker HXSCL) to a sponsored, SEC-registered Nasdaq listing on 10 July 2026 — the qualitative distinction this framework's 2026-07-17 session relies on to treat SKHY's SEC-filed financials as Rule-0-compliant primary-source data rather than a data-sourcing gap. *(New term.)* |
| **AEC-Q100** | An automotive-electronics-industry reliability qualification standard (Automotive Electronics Council) that semiconductor components must pass before an automaker will design them into a vehicle — stress-testing for temperature extremes, vibration, and long service life. Re-qualifying a competing part against this standard typically takes 2+ years, a documented mechanism behind the "switching costs" Moat Signal cited for STMicroelectronics (STM) in this framework's 2026-07-19 session. *(New term.)* |
| **AGCM (Autorità Garante della Concorrenza e del Mercato)** | Italy's national competition/antitrust regulator. Levied a €256M fine against Ryanair Holdings (RYAAY) in Q3 FY26 for alleged anti-competitive conduct — under appeal, with an €85M (~33%) exceptional provision included against it in Ryanair's FY26 (year ended March 31, 2026) results, per this framework's 2026-07-19 RYAAY session. *(New term.)* |
| **AlixPartners** | A large management-consulting firm specializing in corporate restructuring and turnaround advisory — engaging it for an operational review is a routine step for a company managing a difficult liquidity position, and is not, on its own, evidence that bankruptcy is imminent or even under active board consideration. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 LCID session, where a 14 July 2026 report claimed Lucid Group had engaged AlixPartners to evaluate a bankruptcy/take-private filing — a claim the company publicly and specifically denied ("has not recommended bankruptcy to management or the Board"). *(New term.)* |
| **AML (Anti-Money Laundering)** | Laws and internal controls designed to stop criminal proceeds from moving through legitimate financial channels. An AML investigation is a regulator/prosecutor probe into whether a company's controls failed to catch suspicious activity — a fundamental (not price-action) risk this framework tracks under Rule 9's mandatory re-valuation triggers. |
| **Ancillary revenue** | An airline's non-ticket revenue — priority boarding, reserved seats, baggage fees, car hire, travel insurance, and similar add-ons — distinct from "scheduled revenue" (the base fare). Ryanair's ancillary revenue rose 6% to €4.99BN (€24/passenger) in FY2026, cited in this framework's 2026-07-19 RYAAY session. *(New term.)* |
| **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** | The annualized run-rate value of a subscription/recurring-revenue business's contracted revenue at a point in time (e.g. a product doing $25M/quarter of recurring revenue has a $100M ARR run-rate). Useful for tracking a fast-growing product line (e.g. Adobe's Firefly AI generative-credits business) before it's large enough to break out as its own reported segment — a qualitative growth/TAM-expansion signal, not itself a scored input. |
| **ASIC (Application-Specific Integrated Circuit)** | A chip custom-designed for one specific task (e.g. Google's TPU, Amazon's Trainium) rather than a general-purpose processor like a GPU — the main long-term competitive threat cited against NVIDIA's GPU-centric AI-compute franchise in this framework's 2026-07-05 NVDA session, since a hyperscaler that designs its own ASIC can reduce reliance on merchant GPU suppliers for that specific workload. |
| **ASP (Average Selling Price)** | The average price a company sells a unit of its product for (e.g. iPhone ASP) — a pricing-power signal distinct from unit volume; sustained ASP/premium-tier share without losing overall unit volume is evidence of brand-premium moat strength, as used for AAPL's Moat Signal in this framework's 2026-07-06 session. |
| **ASU 2016-01** | The US GAAP accounting standards update (effective 2018) requiring companies to run unrealized gains/losses on equity-security investments through net income every period, rather than through other comprehensive income as under the prior rule. The reason Berkshire Hathaway's GAAP "net earnings" swings sharply with stock-market moves (e.g. a $22.8B net *loss* in FY2022) even when its actual operating businesses are performing consistently — Buffett has repeatedly told investors to look at operating earnings instead. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-15 BRK session. *(New term.)* |
| **ATM (at-the-money) straddle** | Buying a call and a put at the same strike (closest to the current price) and expiration. Its combined cost is a market-priced estimate of how far a stock is expected to move by that expiration — used in this framework's speculation module as a live cross-check against an implied-volatility-based expected-move calculation. |
| **AUA (Assets Under Administration)** | The total value of client assets a platform administers on their behalf (custody, settlement, record-keeping) — a scale/reach metric for a wealth-management or brokerage platform, distinct from the platform's own revenue or profit. Cited for iFAST Corporation (SGX:AIY) in this framework's 2026-07-11 APAC-EX-JP screening session. |
| **AUC (Assets Under Custody)** | The total value of client securities/assets a bank or custodian holds and safekeeps on clients' behalf (settlement, record-keeping, asset servicing) but does not own — a scale/switching-cost metric for a custody/securities-services business, distinct from the bank's own balance sheet. JPMorgan reported a record $38 trillion in AUC as of Q2 2025, cited as Moat Signal "switching costs" evidence in this framework's 2026-07-14 JPM session. |
| **AUV (Average Unit Volume)** | The average annual sales generated by a single restaurant/store location — a scale/unit-economics metric used to compare a chain's per-location productivity against smaller competitors or the industry average. Cited as Moat Signal "scale cost advantage" evidence for McDonald's (~$2.8M AUV vs. a ~$1M fast-food-industry average) in this framework's 2026-07-10 MCD Quality Score addendum. |
| **AV (Autonomous Vehicle)** | A self-driving vehicle; "robotaxi" refers specifically to an AV operated as an on-demand ride-hailing service. Uber partners with (rather than builds) AV developers — e.g. Rivian, WeRide, MOIA — to deploy AVs on its platform, cited as Growth sub-score TAM-expansion context in this framework's 2026-07-05 UBER session. Uber's robotaxi hardware partnership uses Lucid Group (LCID) vehicles fitted with NVIDIA compute/sensor technology — the origin of the Telegram post that triggered this framework's 2026-07-14 LCID session — but this partnership is not itself a scored financial input under Rule 0. |
| **Ayar Third Investment Company (Ayar)** | Lucid Group's (LCID) controlling shareholder, an investment vehicle affiliated with Saudi Arabia's Public Investment Fund (PIF) — the sovereign entity that has repeatedly recapitalized Lucid via privately-placed redeemable convertible preferred stock (Series A/B/C) and an upsized delayed draw term loan facility (see **DDTL** below). Cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 LCID session as the primary reason Lucid's balance-sheet distress hasn't (yet) forced a market-priced dilutive raise or a restructuring — its continued willingness to fund the company is a key, non-guaranteed assumption underlying Lucid's stated liquidity runway. *(New term.)* |
| **Backlog** | The dollar value of signed customer orders not yet recognized as revenue — a forward demand-visibility signal for hardware/equipment makers with long order-to-delivery cycles. Ciena's backlog grew >$600M sequentially to a record $7.7B in Q2 FY2026, with management stating >80% of current hardware backlog is expected to convert to revenue within 12 months — cited as documented TAM-expansion evidence for the Growth sub-score's +10 modifier in this framework's CIEN sessions. |
| **Benchmark EBIT / Benchmark EPS** | Experian's own non-GAAP presentation basis for operating profit and earnings per share — reported alongside statutory (GAAP) figures, adjusted for items management deems non-operational (e.g. acquisition-related amortization, exceptional items). Cited from the company's own results announcements in this framework's EXPN sessions; not directly comparable to a GAAP-derived EBIT computed from filed financials without the same add-backs, similar to the "Adjusted EBITDA" caveat above. |
| **Beta** | A stock's sensitivity to overall market moves — a beta of 1.0 means the stock tends to move in line with the market; above 1.0 means more volatile, below 1.0 means less. Used as an input (alongside the risk-free rate and Equity Risk Premium) to estimate a company's cost of equity in a DCF's WACC calculation. |
| **BHE (Berkshire Hathaway Energy)** | Berkshire Hathaway's regulated utility/energy holding-company subsidiary (electric/gas utilities, transmission, and a large renewables portfolio) — one of Berkshire's core operating segments alongside insurance, BNSF, and Manufacturing/Service/Retailing. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-15 BRK session. *(New term.)* |
| **Bitcoin / Digital assets** | SpaceX (via its X/Twitter-era balance sheet, now consolidated under common-control accounting — see Common-control accounting below) holds 18,712 BTC, fair-valued at $1,637M as of 31 Dec 2025 (S-1/A Note 7) — a balance-sheet holding disclosed for completeness in this framework's 2026-07-13 SPCX session, not itself a scored input. |
| **BNSF** | Burlington Northern Santa Fe, the Class I freight railroad Berkshire Hathaway wholly owns — one of only two major Class I railroads serving the Western United States (the other being Union Pacific), a structural near-duopoly. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-15 BRK session. *(New term.)* |
| **Brent (crude)** | The international benchmark price for crude oil (sourced from North Sea production), used alongside WTI (West Texas Intermediate, the US benchmark) to price and discuss oil-company economics. A company's own disclosed "breakeven Brent price" (the oil price at which capex and dividends are fully funded by operating cash flow) is a self-reported cost-efficiency metric, not directly comparable across companies or third-party industry figures unless the same basis (e.g. capex+dividend vs. capex-only, corporate-wide vs. new-well) is confirmed — flagged as an imperfect comparison in this framework's 2026-07-07 CVX Quality Score addendum. |
| **bps (basis points)** | 1 bps = 0.01 percentage points. 50 bps = 0.5%. |
| **Buyback yield (net buyback yield)** | The rate at which a company's share count shrinks per year from repurchasing its own stock (net of any new shares issued, e.g. for employee compensation) — a component of shareholder yield. |
| **BVNK** | A stablecoin-payments-infrastructure startup Mastercard agreed to acquire for up to $1.8B (announced 17 March 2026) — still pending regulatory approval as of this framework's 2026-07-09 MA session, expected to close late 2026. Cited as a directional (not-yet-realized) TAM-expansion vector in MA's Growth sub-score. |
| **CAGR** | Compound Annual Growth Rate — the smoothed yearly growth rate that gets you from a start value to an end value over several years. |
| **Capital rotation** | Investors shifting money out of one asset/sector and into another in anticipation of relatively better returns elsewhere — e.g. a newly-listed direct competitor's IPO drawing buying interest (and potentially selling pressure on incumbents) as investors reallocate within the same sector. Cited as one candidate driver (alongside broader AI-capex-spending caution and a short-seller's public bearish thesis) behind MU's sharp early-July 2026 pullback, ahead of SK Hynix's (SKHY) planned 10 July 2026 Nasdaq listing, in this framework's 2026-07-08 MU re-trigger session. |
| **Common-control accounting (ASC 805-50)** | The GAAP rule requiring two commonly-controlled entities to have their financial statements retrospectively combined "as if" always combined, rather than using acquisition/purchase accounting — the reason SpaceX's (SPCX) FY2023–FY2025 audited financials already include xAI's and X's (Twitter's) results even before their formal 2025/2026 merger dates, since Elon Musk controlled all three entities throughout. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-13 SPCX session, where it materially explains several line items (AI/advertising revenue present in pre-merger years, a large FY2023 impairment, a Bitcoin balance-sheet holding). |
| **Covered call** | An options-income strategy: selling (writing) a call option against shares already owned, collecting the option's premium as income in exchange for capping upside if the stock rises above the call's strike price before it expires. "Covered" means the shares held fully back the obligation, vs. a riskier "naked" call sold without owning the underlying stock. This framework holds STIM shares specifically to write covered calls against for income. |
| **C2M (Consumer-to-Manufacturer)** | A business model that connects buyers directly with factories/manufacturers, bypassing traditional distribution and inventory intermediaries — reduces handling/inventory costs and underpins Pinduoduo/PDD's low-price cost-advantage moat. |
| **CET1 (Common Equity Tier 1) capital ratio** | A bank regulatory-capital adequacy measure: core equity capital (common stock plus retained earnings, minus most intangibles) divided by risk-weighted assets — the primary metric bank regulators use to judge a depository institution's loss-absorbing capacity, distinct from this framework's own Net Debt/EBITDA leverage gate, which doesn't apply to a bank (deposits, not "debt," fund the balance sheet). Cited as context in this framework's 2026-07-12 BAC session (CET1 ~13.1% as of Q3 2025, well above regulatory minimums) to explain why the framework's standard Balance Sheet sub-score is not meaningful for a depository institution, not as a scored input itself. |
| **CET1 (Common Equity Tier 1) ratio** | A bank's core regulatory capital-adequacy measure: its highest-quality capital (common equity) divided by its risk-weighted assets. Regulators set a minimum a bank must stay above; the cushion above that minimum is a direct measure of balance-sheet strength — the bank-industry equivalent of this framework's Net Debt/EBITDA balance-sheet check, though not directly substitutable into that formula since banks have no EBITDA line. Citigroup's Standardized CET1 ratio was 13.2% at FY2025 year-end and 12.7% at Q1 2026, both comfortably above its ~11.6% regulatory minimum — cited in this framework's 2026-07-12 C session. |
| **CFR (Corporate Family Rating)** | Moody's rating of a company's overall corporate-family (consolidated) credit risk, distinct from ratings on its specific individual debt instruments (e.g. particular bond issues). FICO's CFR is Ba2, sub-investment grade — the rating this framework's Upgrade 5 asset-light exception checks against. |
| **Chapter 11 (bankruptcy)** | The section of the US Bankruptcy Code covering business reorganization — lets a financially distressed company keep operating while restructuring its debts under court supervision (distinct from Chapter 7, which liquidates the company entirely). A company "considering" or "weighing" a Chapter 11 filing is not the same as having filed one — an unconfirmed report and a company's own on-record denial of it are each just a claim, not a settled fact, until an actual filing (or its confirmed absence) is independently verified. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 UBER session regarding unconfirmed, company-denied bankruptcy rumors about AV-partner Lucid Group (LCID). |
| **CID (Civil Investigative Demand)** | A formal, subpoena-like investigative tool a state Attorney General (or the FTC) can use to compel a company to produce documents and information *before* any lawsuit is filed — an investigation-stage step, not itself an enforcement action or a finding of wrongdoing. Florida's AG issued one to FICO around 2 Jul 2026 as part of a state antitrust investigation into its credit-scoring pricing practices. |
| **Continued listing standard** | An exchange's (e.g. NYSE's) ongoing minimum requirements — market capitalization, stockholders' equity, share price — that a company must keep meeting *after* its IPO/listing to remain listed. Falling short triggers a compliance notice and a cure period (which can run up to 18 months), not immediate suspension or delisting. NYSE Section 802.01B requires average global market capitalization and stockholders' equity of at least $50 million; Wallbox (WBX) was notified of non-compliance under this rule on 2026-02-12. |
| **CIK (Central Index Key)** | The unique numeric identifier the SEC assigns to every company or individual that files with EDGAR (e.g. Apple Inc.'s CIK is 0000320193) — used to construct the API/URL paths this framework pulls SEC filings and XBRL data from. |
| **Circana** | An independent market-research firm that publishes restaurant-industry data, including the annual "Definitive U.S. Restaurant Rankings" — a third-party (non-company-sourced) citation basis for average-unit-volume (AUV) comparisons across chains, distinct from a company's own self-reported per-unit sales figures. Cited (via Restaurant Dive) as Moat Signal "scale cost advantage" evidence for The Cheesecake Factory's #1 industry AUV ranking ($12.8M/unit) in this framework's 2026-07-17 CAKE session. *(New term.)* |
| **CER (Constant Exchange Rate)** | A growth-rate presentation that strips out the effect of currency movements by re-calculating a prior period's results at the *current* period's exchange rates — isolates real underlying business growth from FX noise. Multinational non-US filers (e.g. Novo Nordisk, reporting in DKK) routinely guide and report growth "at CER" alongside the as-reported figure; this framework treats CER growth as the more decision-relevant read of underlying trend, distinct from a currency-driven swing. |
| **Cross-border payments** | Sending money between people or businesses in different countries/currencies — the core product category for remittance and international-transfer fintechs. |
| **Convertible senior notes** | A bond that can be converted into a fixed number of the issuer's shares (at a preset conversion/strike price) instead of being repaid in cash at maturity, letting a company borrow at a lower (sometimes 0%) coupon in exchange for giving investors that upside option. Ciena priced (9 Jun 2026) and closed (~11 Jun 2026, after full exercise of the initial purchasers' option) $2.875B of 0.00%-coupon convertible senior notes due 2031, with an associated $1,000/share conversion strike and note-hedge ("capped call") transactions to limit dilution; disclosed net-proceeds use: ~$100M to fund the note-hedge cost, ~$140M to repurchase ~0.3M shares, ~$1.14B to repay the existing term loan, and the remainder for general corporate purposes including supply-chain capacity investment. A material capital-structure event (Rule 9) not yet reflected in the Q2 FY2026 (quarter ended 2 May 2026) reported balance sheet, since it closed after that quarter's close. |
| **Dasher** | DoorDash's term for the independent-contractor courier who fulfills a delivery order on its marketplace — the third (supply) side of DoorDash's consumer/merchant/Dasher three-sided network. |
| **DashPass** | DoorDash's paid subscription program ($9.99/month or $96/year as of 2026) offering $0 delivery fees and reduced service fees on qualifying orders — a documented switching-cost/loyalty mechanism (annual-plan lock-in, order-frequency increase among subscribers) cited as Moat Signal evidence in this framework's DASH sessions. |
| **Dell'Oro Group** | An independent market-research firm covering telecom/networking equipment; its quarterly Optical Transport market-share reports are a third-party (non-company-sourced) citation basis for Moat Signal "market share" evidence in this framework, e.g. Ciena's ~50% US optical-transport share and largest 2025 share gain (+3pp) among major vendors. |
| **DCI (Data Center Interconnect)** | Networking equipment/technology that links geographically separate data centers over optical fiber at high bandwidth — the specific optical-networking sub-segment seeing the fastest growth from hyperscaler AI buildouts, and where Ciena ranks among the top revenue-share vendors alongside Nokia and Cisco per Dell'Oro Group. |
| **DDTL (Delayed Draw Term Loan)** | A loan facility where the lender commits to a maximum principal amount upfront, but the borrower draws it down in one or more tranches over time (rather than receiving it all at once) — gives the borrower flexibility to raise only what it needs, when needed, without a fresh negotiation each time. Lucid Group's (LCID) DDTL Credit Facility with its controlling shareholder Ayar Third Investment Company was upsized from $750M to $1.98B in November 2025, then further increased to ~$2.5B in April 2026 (with $500M drawn that month) — cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 LCID session as evidence of the company's continuing reliance on its controlling shareholder for incremental liquidity. *(New term.)* |
| **Core (non-GAAP measure)** | PepsiCo's own non-GAAP presentation basis for operating profit and EPS, stripping out items management deems non-recurring (mark-to-market gains/losses, restructuring charges, impairments, acquisition/divestiture-related charges) — reported alongside GAAP figures, same "company's own adjusted metric" caveat as Adjusted EBITDA/AOI/Benchmark EBIT above. In PepsiCo's Q2 2026 release, GAAP EPS rose 137% YoY (helped by an easy prior-year comp: Q2 2025 included Rockstar/Be Cheery impairment charges), while Core EPS — the cleaner read of underlying operating performance — rose only 4%, and Core *constant-currency* EPS just 1%. This framework scores off filed GAAP figures, not Core, per the "guidance/self-reported non-GAAP metrics are not scored" principle, but Core is cited qualitatively here to show the GAAP headline growth rate is not representative of underlying trend. |
| **Coupon (bond)** | The fixed annual interest rate a bond pays on its face value, expressed as a percentage — e.g. a €1.0bn bond with a 3.875% coupon pays €38.75M/year in interest. A notional-weighted average across a company's full slate of outstanding bonds gives a directly computed cost-of-debt figure rather than an estimate anchored to just one or two bonds' coupons, as used for Deutsche Börse (DB1) in this framework's 2026-07-07 session (9 bonds, weighted-average coupon 2.640%). |

| **CapEx** | Capital Expenditure — money spent buying or upgrading physical assets (factories, equipment, data centers). |

| **DCF** | Discounted Cash Flow — a valuation method that estimates a company's worth today by projecting its future cash and discounting it back to present-day value. |
| **DDM** | Dividend Discount Model — a valuation method that values a company based on the dividends it's expected to pay out. |
| **Deferred tax valuation allowance release** | A one-off GAAP accounting event where a company reverses a prior write-down on its deferred tax assets (e.g. carried-forward losses from earlier unprofitable years) once it judges those assets are now likely usable — common when a recently-unprofitable company turns durably profitable. | **Digital-asset (crypto) treasury strategy** | A corporate strategy of raising capital (via equity, debt, or both) specifically to acquire and hold cryptocurrency (e.g. Bitcoin) as a balance-sheet reserve asset, distinct from holding crypto as an operating/product input — popularized by Strategy Inc. Because the resulting holdings are marked to fair value each period and run through *operating* income (not treated as a financing/investing-only item), swings in crypto prices directly and materially move reported operating income and net income — Trump Media's own FY2025 10-K states it ended the year with ~$2,473.2M of combined cash/investments/digital-asset holdings against ~$947.1M of debt. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-17 DJT session, where a −$403.2M FY2025 and −$244.0M Q1 2026 unrealized/realized digital-asset loss (tagged `CryptoAssetRealizedAndUnrealizedGainLossOperating` in the company's own SEC XBRL filings) is the single largest driver of both periods' operating losses. *(New term.)* |
| **Dilutive (capital raise)** | Raising money by issuing new shares, which shrinks (dilutes) each existing shareholder's ownership percentage. |
| **Direct listing** | A way for a company to go public by listing existing shares directly on an exchange, without the traditional IPO process of issuing new shares through underwriters at a set offer price. Results in a shorter trailing public financial/price history than a longer-listed company — relevant when a Phase 01 quality-gate condition requires a multi-year lookback (e.g. the Turnaround Sub-Gate's 10-year historical-ROIC check) the company may not have enough history to ever satisfy. |
| **Discontinued operations** | A line item in financial statements that separates out the results of a business unit the company has sold, shut down, or classified as held-for-sale, so it no longer mixes with "continuing operations." When prior-period comparatives are restated to exclude a newly-discontinued unit (e.g. CD Projekt's FY2024 figures restated to exclude GOG.COM following its divestment), year-over-year ratios computed on the restated basis aren't directly comparable to figures from the original, non-restated filing — the basis used must be stated explicitly. |
| **DSA (Digital Services Act)** | An EU regulation requiring online platforms to identify, assess, and mitigate risks from illegal/unsafe content and products, with fines of up to 6% of global annual revenue for breaches. | **DB-Engines ranking** | An independent monthly popularity ranking of database management systems, based on a blend of search-engine query volume, job listings, technical-forum mentions, and social-media chatter — a third-party (non-company-sourced) proxy for installed-base scale and mindshare, distinct from a revenue-based market-share figure. | **DLC (Dual-Listed Company)** | A corporate structure where two separately-listed legal entities operate as a single combined economic business with shared management and combined consolidated financial reporting, while each entity's shares trade separately on an exchange. Consistent with this framework's "guidance/self-reported metrics are not scored" principle, a preannouncement's headline numbers (e.g. revenue, EPS) are used here only as qualitative, dated, primary-sourced context for *why* a price moved — never plugged into the Quality Score's trailing-financial-statement sub-scores, which wait for the complete, final quarterly filing. | **Effective tax rate** | The actual percentage of a company's pretax income paid as income tax in a given period (tax provision ÷ pretax income) — distinct from the statutory tax rate, and can swing sharply (even to unusually high or negative levels) when pretax income is near zero, as seen in CSGP's TTM figures in this framework's 2026-07-04 session. |

| **Equity Risk Premium (ERP)** | The extra return equity investors demand, on average, for holding stocks instead of risk-free government bonds — an assumed modeling input (not a directly observable fact), used with the risk-free rate and Beta to estimate a company's cost of equity in a DCF's WACC calculation. |
| **EUV (Extreme Ultraviolet Lithography)** | The most advanced chip-manufacturing lithography technology, using 13.5-nanometer-wavelength light to print the smallest, most advanced transistor patterns (5nm/3nm/2nm nodes). | **EV** | Enterprise Value — a company's total value to all capital providers: market cap + debt − cash. |

| **EV/Revenue** | Enterprise Value divided by revenue — used for high-growth or pre-profit companies where earnings-based multiples don't work yet. |

| **Historical (realized) volatility** | How much a stock has actually moved over a recent trailing period (this framework typically uses 30 days), annualized — the backward-looking counterpart to implied volatility. |
| **Hurdle rate** | The minimum acceptable annual return for an investment to be worth making — this framework uses 10% as the hurdle the Upside/Downside Modifier measures expected return against. |
| **IDC (International Data Corporation)** | An independent global market-research and analyst firm covering the technology industry, including quarterly DRAM/NAND/HBM memory-chip supplier revenue and market-share tracking — a third-party (non-company-sourced) citation basis for Moat Signal "market share" evidence in this framework, analogous to Dell'Oro Group's role for telecom equipment or TrendForce's for the broader memory sector. | **IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards)** | The accounting standard most non-US companies (including Stellantis) use for their audited financial statements, as opposed to US GAAP. | **Implied volatility (IV)** | The options market's own forward-looking estimate of how much a stock might move, expressed as an annualized percentage — higher IV means the market is pricing more uncertainty, typically around an unresolved event (earnings, an unconfirmed report, litigation). | **Installed Base Management (IBM) sales** | ASML's own term for its recurring service/upgrade/field-option revenue line (net service and field option sales) generated from lithography systems already installed at customer sites — distinct from "New systems" (net system sales), the one-off sale of a new tool. | **Interest coverage (ratio)** | EBIT ÷ interest expense — how many times over a company could pay its interest bill from operating profit; higher means less balance-sheet risk from debt. |
| **Inventory correction (destocking)** | A cyclical phase where customers (distributors, OEMs) that over-ordered during a supply shortage or demand spike work down their own excess stockpiles by ordering less than they're actually consuming — temporarily depressing a supplier's reported revenue well below underlying end-demand until the excess inventory clears. A standard, recurring feature of the semiconductor industry's boom-bust cycle, distinct from a structural, competitively-driven decline (e.g. losing share to a rival). Cited as the qualitative basis for treating STMicroelectronics' (STM) FY2024–FY2025 revenue decline as cyclical rather than structural in this framework's 2026-07-19 session, based on analyst-consensus estimates of a sharp FY2026–FY2027 growth snap-back. *(New term.)* |
| **Invested Capital** | The total capital (debt + equity, sometimes netted for cash) that has been put to work in a business — the denominator in a Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) calculation. This framework nets out cash (debt + equity − cash) when computing it, consistent with how Net Debt/EBITDA already nets cash from gross debt. |
| **Investment grade** | A credit rating (BBB-/Baa3 or higher) signaling a low perceived risk of default — as opposed to "junk" / high-yield ratings below that line. |
| **IP Box** | A preferential corporate tax regime (Poland's version is one example) that taxes profit derived from qualifying intellectual property — patents, copyrighted software/game source code, and similar — at a reduced rate (e.g. 5%) instead of the standard corporate rate. Explains an unusually low effective tax rate for IP-heavy companies like game studios without implying any accounting irregularity, provided the regime and qualifying income are disclosed. |
| **IPO (Initial Public Offering)** | The traditional process by which a private company first sells shares to the public on a stock exchange, typically underwritten by investment banks at a set offer price (vs. a direct listing, which skips the underwriting step). A recent IPO leaves a company with a short trailing public price/financial history, which can make multi-year lookback checks (5yr historical PE range, the Turnaround Sub-Gate's 10-year ROIC lookback, a clean 3+ year revenue/EPS growth base) impossible to compute over a full window — see Direct listing above for the related data-gap issue. |
| **IRR** | Internal Rate of Return — the annualized percentage return an investment is expected to generate. |
| **IV crush** | A sharp drop in implied volatility once the event driving it resolves (confirmed, denied, or simply passes), which can reduce an option's value even when the underlying stock moves in the anticipated direction — a risk specific to buying options around a known event. |
| **Kelley Blue Book (KBB) Best Resale Value Award** | An annual US automotive-industry award (and associated retained-value study) recognizing vehicle brands/models with the highest projected resale value after a 5-year ownership period — cited as third-party, cited evidence for a Moat Signal "brand premium" checklist row (e.g. Toyota's 6-consecutive-year win, Lexus's 5-consecutive-year luxury-category win, in this framework's 2026-07-12 TM session). |

| **Managed care** | A health-insurance model (Medicare Advantage, Medicaid managed care, employer-sponsored HMO/PPO plans) where a private insurer administers care and payment on a government program's or employer's behalf, in exchange for a fixed or risk-adjusted payment, as distinct from traditional fee-for-service insurance. | **MAPC (Monthly Active Platform Consumers)** | The number of unique consumers who used at least one Uber offering (Mobility or Delivery) in a given month — Uber's core platform-reach metric, distinct from Gross Bookings (a dollar-volume metric) or MAU (a generic active-user count used by other companies). |

| **MCR (Medical Care Ratio) / MLR (Medical Loss Ratio)** | The percentage of premium revenue a health insurer pays out in medical claims (MCR is UnitedHealth Group's own term; MLR is the more common industry/regulatory term, also used in an Affordable Care Act minimum-payout-ratio rule). | **Money-center bank** | A large, internationally active bank (e.g. Citigroup, JPMorgan, Bank of America) that raises most of its funding from wholesale/institutional sources and global capital markets rather than primarily local retail deposits — distinct from a smaller regional or community bank. | **MOPS (Market Observation Post System)** | Taiwan's official corporate-disclosure/regulatory-filing system (run by the Taiwan Stock Exchange) — the Taiwanese equivalent of the US SEC's EDGAR. | **MSCI** | Morgan Stanley Capital International — an index provider; "MSCI QUAL" / "MSCI World" etc. refer to specific indices it publishes. |
| **Multi-homing** | When customers on one or both sides of a platform routinely use multiple competing platforms at once rather than committing to just one (e.g. rail travelers keeping several ticketing apps installed and price-comparing across them) — a documented mechanism that dilutes an otherwise-plausible network effect, since neither side is locked into a single platform's ecosystem. | **NAV** | Net Asset Value — the value of a company's (or fund's) assets minus its liabilities. |
| **Negative stockholders' equity (shareholders' deficit)** | When a company's total liabilities exceed its total assets, producing a negative "Total shareholders' equity" balance-sheet line — commonly the result of funding large share buybacks with debt faster than the company retains earnings. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-13 UNH session. |
| **Organic revenue growth** | Revenue growth excluding the effects of acquisitions, disposals, and currency movements (i.e. constant-currency growth of the business as it stood at the start of the period) — the growth-quality metric companies like Experian guide and report alongside total (as-reported) revenue growth, isolating underlying demand from M&A and FX noise. |
| **Own cash** | A payments/fintech company's *own* corporate cash, as distinct from customer/client balances it holds in transit. Matters because a payments company's reported "cash and cash equivalents" balance-sheet line often conflates the two — using the conflated figure overstates net cash and understates Enterprise Value. |
| **Owner Earnings** | Warren Buffett's adjusted cash-flow measure: Net Income + D&A − *Maintenance* CapEx only (excludes growth CapEx) — used instead of raw FCF for moat-building reinvestors like MSFT, GOOGL, META, AMZN (Hybrid Upgrade 1). |
| **Partial fill** | When a limit order executes for less than its full requested quantity (e.g. the broker fills 600 of an intended ~1,553-share order) because not enough shares were available at the limit price before the order was replaced, cancelled, or the market moved away — leaving the position below its full target size until a follow-up order completes it. |
| **Payers** | Match Group's own disclosed metric: the average number of users across a period who paid for at least one of its subscription or à la carte products — its core paying-user KPI, distinct from Monthly Active Users (which includes non-paying users) or revenue itself. Reported per brand (Tinder, Hinge, Evergreen & Emerging, MG Asia). Cited in this framework's 2026-07-16 MTCH session: total portfolio Payers declined every year from FY2023 (15,602K) through FY2025 (14,165K), driven mainly by Tinder's own Payers falling ~7% YoY in both FY2024 and FY2025. *(New term.)* |
| **P/B (Price-to-Book)** | Price ÷ book value (accounting net worth) per share — common for valuing banks and financials. |
| **PBM (Pharmacy Benefit Manager)** | A company that administers prescription-drug benefits on behalf of health plans and employers, negotiating manufacturer rebates and pharmacy reimbursement rates. Three PBMs — CVS Caremark, Cigna's Express Scripts, and UnitedHealth Group's Optum Rx — together process roughly 80% of US prescription-drug claims, a concentrated market structure cited as Moat Signal "scale cost advantage" evidence in this framework's 2026-07-13 UNH session. |
| **P/FCF (Price-to-Free-Cash-Flow)** | Market capitalization ÷ Free Cash Flow — an earnings-multiple analog used as a scenario-valuation tool when a company's GAAP EPS is too distorted by one-off items to build a reliable EPS×PE scenario set, as in this framework's 2026-07-05 UBER session. |
| **PIK (Payment-in-Kind) interest** | Interest a borrower pays by adding to the amount owed (increasing principal) rather than paying cash — common in distressed-company debt restructurings where the borrower can't afford cash interest payments. Grows total debt over time rather than reducing it, the opposite of a normal cash-pay loan. |
| **PIPE (Private Investment in Public Equity)** | A private placement of newly-issued stock (and sometimes convertible debt) sold directly to a small group of institutional investors, often used to raise a large amount of capital quickly — common around or after a SPAC merger, without the disclosure timeline of a fully marketed public offering. Trump Media & Technology Group (DJT) closed a ~$2.44B PIPE-plus-convertible-note raise on 30 May 2025 (common stock plus $1.0B of 0.00%-coupon convertible senior secured notes due 2028) specifically to fund its bitcoin/digital-asset treasury strategy — cited in this framework's 2026-07-17 DJT session. *(New term.)* |
| **P/TBV (Price-to-Tangible-Book-Value)** | Like P/B, but using *tangible* book value (book value minus intangible assets such as goodwill) — a standard bank-valuation multiple. |
| **PCAOB (Public Company Accounting Oversight Board)** | The US regulator overseeing audits of public companies. An S-1 registrant's financial statements (e.g. SpaceX's FY2023–FY2025 figures, audited by PricewaterhouseCoopers LLP) must carry a PCAOB-standard audit opinion just like an existing public filer's, even before the company has actually gone public — cited in this framework's 2026-07-13 SPCX session. |
| **PDK (Process Design Kit)** | A foundry-supplied set of design rules, models, and libraries a chip design is built around — switching foundries means redesigning a chip around a new PDK, cited as switching-cost Moat Signal evidence in this framework's 2026-07-12 TSM session. |
| **PE (Price-to-Earnings) ratio** | Share price ÷ earnings per share — the most common "how expensive is this stock" multiple. |
| **PEG ratio** | PE ratio ÷ earnings growth rate — a PE adjusted for growth, used to judge whether a fast grower's multiple is justified by its growth rate. |
| **pp (percentage points)** | A direct difference between two percentages (e.g. margin falling from 42% to 39% is a 3pp drop) — distinct from a "%" *change*, which would describe that same move as -7%. |
| **PFNA / PBNA (PepsiCo Foods North America / PepsiCo Beverages North America)** | PepsiCo's two US/Canada reporting segments — PFNA covers convenient foods (Frito-Lay, Quaker), PBNA covers beverages (Pepsi, Gatorade, and 2025-acquired brands). In Q2 2026, PFNA organic volume fell ~2% and PBNA organic volume fell ~6% even as PBNA's *reported* revenue grew (acquisitions and pricing offset the volume decline) — a documented North America volume-softness signal cited in this framework's 2026-07-09 PEP session, distinct from PepsiCo's stronger-performing international segments (International Beverages Franchise, Europe/Middle East/Africa, Latin America Foods, Asia Pacific Foods). |
| **PP&E (Property, Plant & Equipment)** | Physical, long-lived business assets — land, buildings, manufacturing facilities, and equipment — one of the two components (alongside intangible-asset spend, e.g. licensing/capitalized development costs) that can make up a company's total capital expenditure; splitting the two matters when only one component is captured by a data vendor's "CapEx" field, as found for NVO in this framework's 2026-07-05 session. |
| **PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)** | A long-term contract (often 15–20+ years) under which a buyer agrees to purchase power from a supplier at agreed terms — e.g. Chevron and Microsoft's June 2026 20-year agreement for Chevron to supply gas-fired power from a dedicated West Texas plant to a Microsoft data center. Creates real contractual lock-in/switching costs for the buyer, but for the seller represents committed capital and revenue only once the underlying project reaches FID, not merely upon signing. |
| **Pre-revenue (company)** | A company that has not yet generated any revenue from commercial sales of a product or service, typically because its core product is still in development, testing, or regulatory approval rather than being sold to customers. Distinct from an early-stage but revenue-generating company; standard revenue-based ratios (gross margin, revenue CAGR, net margin) are mathematically undefined or trivially zero for a pre-revenue company — a structural characteristic (confirmed via a complete absence of any revenue-recognition XBRL tag in SEC filings) rather than a missing data point to be filled in. Oklo Inc. (OKLO) is an example, cited in this framework's 2026-07-16 session — $0 total revenue in every fiscal year 2022–2025. *(New term.)* |
| **Prepaid Forward Contract** | An equity derivative in which a company prepays a financial-institution counterparty for the future delivery of its own shares — typically entered alongside a convertible-note issuance to offset dilution, similar in spirit to a capped call transaction. Marked to fair value each period, so a rising share price produces a large unrealized (non-cash) accounting gain unrelated to operating performance. IREN's Prepaid Forward Contracts produced a $664.993M unrealized gain in a single quarter (Q1 FY2026), the primary driver of that quarter's reported net income — cited in this framework's 2026-07-15 IREN session as an earnings-quality flag. *(New term.)* |
| **Process node** (e.g. "2nm"/"N2", "A16") | A generation of semiconductor manufacturing technology, generally denoted by an approximate feature-size label; each new node typically offers better performance/power/density — cited throughout this framework's 2026-07-12 TSM and ASML sessions. |
| **Pro forma (financial statements)** | Restated or hypothetical financial statements showing how a company's results would have looked under a different structure than what was actually reported — e.g. as if a just-completed spinoff or acquisition had already been in effect for the full historical period shown. Companies separating a division (e.g. S&P Global's Mobility Global spinoff) are required to file pro forma continuing-operations statements, but typically with a short lag after the separation closes — until they're filed, trailing "as-reported" financials still consolidate the divested unit and shouldn't be paired uncritically with a post-separation share price/market cap (see the **Spin-off** entry below). |
| **PT (Price Target)** | An analyst's forecast of where a stock's price will be at a future date. |
| **PUE (Power Usage Effectiveness)** | A data-center efficiency metric: total facility power draw ÷ power actually delivered to computing equipment. Lower is better (1.0 = no overhead); hyperscale operators typically achieve ~1.1–1.2 vs. ~1.5–2.0 for smaller/legacy facilities — a genuine per-unit cost-efficiency signal considered (though not ultimately adopted, for lacking a company-specific citation) as Moat Signal "scale cost advantage" evidence in this framework's 2026-07-05 MSFT session. |
| **QMJ (Quality Minus Junk)** | A Fama-French style academic factor that measures the historical excess return of high-quality companies over low-quality ones — used in this framework as a benchmark, not a strategy input. |
| **QoQ (Quarter-over-Quarter)** | A growth-rate comparison against the immediately preceding quarter, rather than the same quarter a year earlier (YoY) — e.g. Foxconn's AI-server rack shipments cited growing 300% QoQ in this framework's 2026-07-06 HNHPF session. |
| **REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust)** | A company that owns/operates income-producing real estate (or real-estate debt) and, in exchange for a preferential tax structure, must distribute most of its taxable income to shareholders as dividends. Its financials don't carry a conventional "Cost of Revenue"/Gross Margin line the way an operating business does, so this framework's gross-margin- and EV/EBIT-based Phase 01 filters don't cleanly apply — REITs are structurally excluded from screening on that basis, not evaluated on a per-name pass/fail basis. |
| **Redomiciliation (redomicile)** | A corporate action that moves a company's legal place of incorporation from one jurisdiction to another (e.g. Panama to Bermuda) without necessarily changing where it operates or trades — typically done alongside a broader corporate restructuring, and changes which jurisdiction's company law governs shareholder rights and constitutional documents going forward. Carnival Corporation Ltd. redomiciled from Panama to Bermuda on 7 May 2026 as part of unifying its dual-listed structure with Carnival plc. |
| **Reverse stock split** | A corporate action that reduces the number of outstanding shares by a fixed ratio (e.g. 1-for-12) while proportionally increasing the per-share price, leaving total market cap unchanged — typically done to lift a depressed share price above an exchange's minimum-listing-price requirement. A real corporate action, not a data error, but it creates a sharp discontinuity in any share-count or per-share time series that must be accounted for rather than treated as a vendor mistake. FUBO executed a 1-for-12 reverse split effective 2026-03-24, confirmed in this framework's 2026-06-29 session via the abrupt drop in yfinance's `get_shares_full()` series (~353.2M to ~29.4M shares) cross-checked against independent news sources. |
| **RNS (Regulatory News Service)** | The London Stock Exchange's official channel for company regulatory announcements (results, M&A, director/management changes, etc.) — the UK equivalent of a US SEC 8-K filing. An "Investegate RNS" citation in this framework means the announcement came from the company's own regulatory disclosure, not press secondary reporting — used for qualitative "what happened" narrative only, never as a source for numbers used in scoring (Rule 0). |
| **REPLACED (order status)** | An IBKR order-lifecycle status meaning the order was superseded by a new order (e.g. a re-priced limit or a resized quantity) — the original order ID stops being live, but that alone doesn't confirm whether the *replacement* order itself later filled, is still working, or was cancelled; each `REPLACED` order needs its own successor traced to know the real outcome. |
| **R&D (Research & Development)** | Company spending on developing new products/technology, expensed on the income statement (as distinct from CapEx, spent on physical assets) — ASML's sustained R&D spend (~€1.18–1.2B/quarter) is cited as a driver of its EUV moat in this framework's 2026-07-12 session. |
| **Risk adjustment / "upcoding" / "chart review"** | The CMS methodology that pays Medicare Advantage insurers more for enrollees coded with more/higher-severity diagnoses, intended to compensate insurers fairly for sicker patients. "Chart review" is a retrospective audit of a member's medical record to identify additional billable diagnosis codes after the initial claim; congressional oversight (MedPAC) estimated chart reviews drove roughly $24 billion of Medicare Advantage overpayments industry-wide in 2023. "Upcoding" is the disputed/alleged practice of coding more aggressively than a patient's condition supports to increase risk-adjusted payment — the central allegation in the DOJ's ongoing criminal and civil investigation into UnitedHealth Group's Medicare Advantage billing practices, discussed in this framework's 2026-07-13 UNH session. |
| **R/R (Risk/Reward ratio)** | (Expected gain) ÷ (Expected loss) on a trade — this framework requires at least 2:1 before entering. |
| **RAN (Radio Access Network)** | The base-station/radio hardware and software that connects mobile devices to a telecom operator's core network — the largest, most commoditized segment of Nokia's Network Infrastructure business, and the market Dell'Oro/Omdia/Juniper Research track when ranking vendors (Huawei, Ericsson, Nokia, ZTE, Samsung). |
| **RPO (Remaining Performance Obligations) / cRPO (current RPO)** | The total dollar value of contracted-but-not-yet-recognized subscription/services revenue (RPO), and the portion of it expected to be recognized within the next 12 months (cRPO) — a forward-looking demand/backlog metric distinct from trailing revenue, and (when growing faster than revenue) evidence of multi-year contractual lock-in cited as Switching-costs Moat Signal support (e.g. GitLab's Total RPO +18-20% YoY / cRPO +24% YoY through Q1 FY2027). |
| **RPP (Revenue Per Payer)** | Match Group's own disclosed per-user monetization metric: a brand's direct revenue for a period ÷ its Payers in the period ÷ the number of months in the period — functionally an ARPU (average revenue per user) equivalent. Cited in this framework's 2026-07-16 MTCH session as pricing-power evidence (RPP rose across nearly every brand in FY2025, e.g. Tinder +3%, Hinge +7%), though for Tinder — the largest brand — the increase came alongside a simultaneous 7% Payers decline, i.e. a price increase *with* volume loss rather than the "price increases without volume loss" evidentiary bar this framework's Moat Signal checklist requires for a Brand Premium credit. *(New term.)* |
| **Russell Reconstitution / Reclassification** | FTSE Russell's periodic (semi-annual/annual) rebalancing of its Russell index family (e.g. Russell 1000 Growth vs. Value), which can move a stock between style indices based on updated growth/value scoring criteria — a mechanical, systematic-fund-flow event distinct from any change in the underlying business, but capable of producing a real, explainable price move as passive/benchmark-aware capital repositions around the effective date (e.g. GitLab's ~11.5% single-day move after its June 2026 reclassification from Russell Growth to Russell Value indices). |
| **ROCE (Return on Capital Employed)** | A company's own commonly-disclosed profitability metric, closely related to ROIC but not always identically defined (may use a different operating-profit or capital-base convention than this framework's own NOPAT ÷ Invested-Capital calculation). Used in this framework as a cross-check against the framework's self-computed ROIC, not as a substitute for it, when the two diverge — e.g. Experian's own-disclosed FY2026 ROCE of 17.2% vs. this framework's computed ROIC of ~15.6% for the same period. |
| **ROE** | Return on Equity — Net Income ÷ shareholder equity; how efficiently a company generates profit from shareholders' capital. |
| **ROIC** | Return on Invested Capital — how efficiently a company turns the capital invested in it (debt + equity) into profit; a core quality signal in this framework. |
| **ROTCE (Return on Tangible Common Equity)** | Net income available to common shareholders divided by average tangible common equity (common equity minus goodwill/intangibles) — the primary profitability KPI banks themselves report and target, distinct from and typically higher than plain ROE. Citigroup's full-year 2025 ROTCE was 7.7% GAAP (8.8% adjusted), against a 2026 target of 10–11% — cited in this framework's 2026-07-12 C session. |
| **Systemwide sales** | Total sales across all restaurants operating under a chain's brand, franchised and company-operated combined — includes both comparable-sales (like-for-like) growth and new-unit growth, so it can rise faster than comps alone during a store-expansion phase. For a heavily-franchised business like McDonald's, systemwide sales (not consolidated revenue) is the base against which most royalty/rent revenue is earned. |
| **SBC (Stock-Based Compensation)** | Employee pay in the form of company shares or stock options rather than cash. A non-cash expense added back in cash-flow statements (inflating FCF relative to GAAP net income), but a real economic cost to existing shareholders through ongoing dilution — worth tracking alongside FCF/NI conversion and share-count trend, not just the headline FCF figure. |
| **SaaS (Software-as-a-Service)** | A software delivery model where customers pay a recurring subscription to access hosted software rather than buying and installing it outright — the dominant business model for modern enterprise software (e.g. Veeva's Vault/CRM products), valued for its recurring-revenue visibility. |
| **SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market)** | The subset of a company's Total Addressable Market (TAM) that its current products/geography can realistically serve — narrower than TAM, and the figure Nokia discloses for its AI & Cloud segment (€17B, +28% YoY per the FY2025 annual report) when describing that segment's growth runway; distinct from consolidated company revenue, which can still be shrinking even while a sub-segment's SAM expands. |
| **Safeguarded / segregated customer funds** | Money a regulated payments company holds on behalf of its customers (e.g. while a cross-border transfer is in transit), kept in ring-fenced accounts separate from the company's own corporate cash. These balances inflate a payments company's gross balance-sheet cash and, mechanically, its reported operating-cash-flow "change in working capital" line — neither figure represents cash the company can deploy for itself, so both must be stripped out before computing FCF yield, EV, or Net Debt/EBITDA. |
| **Shareholder yield** | Cash returned to shareholders as a percentage of share price — dividend yield plus net buyback yield combined. |
| **Short position / short-seller** | Betting a stock's price will fall by borrowing shares, selling them now, and aiming to buy them back later at a lower price to return to the lender — profiting if the price drops, losing (with theoretically unlimited downside) if it rises instead. A short-seller's publicly stated bearish thesis (e.g. Michael Burry's disclosed short interest cited in press coverage of MU's July 2026 pullback) is a market opinion/risk flag to track, never treated by this framework as verified fact about the underlying business. |
| **SiC (Silicon Carbide)** | A semiconductor material (an alternative to standard silicon) used to make power-electronics components — MOSFETs, diodes — that switch electrical power more efficiently at high voltage/temperature than silicon-based parts, widely used in electric-vehicle drivetrains and industrial power supplies. STMicroelectronics (STM) is the market-leading SiC power-device supplier by revenue share (32.6%, TrendForce 2023 data), cited as Moat Signal "market share" evidence in this framework's 2026-07-19 STM session. *(New term.)* |
| **SMR (Small Modular Reactor)** | A class of nuclear fission reactor designs that are smaller, factory-fabricated, and intended to be simpler/faster to build than a traditional large nuclear power plant — a nuclear-industry technology category distinct from (though related to) a "microreactor" (an even smaller SMR sub-class, typically under ~20 MWe, aimed at single-site/off-grid power). Oklo Inc.'s (OKLO) Aurora powerhouse is a microreactor-class SMR design, still pre-commercial as of this framework's 2026-07-16 session (first unit targeted for commercial operation in 2027–2028). *(New term.)* |
| **Scheme of arrangement** | A UK/Bermuda-law court-approved procedure that lets a company restructure shareholders' or creditors' rights (e.g. exchange one class of shares for another) with a binding vote of the affected class, rather than needing unanimous individual consent — the legal mechanism Carnival plc used to exchange its shares 1-for-1 into Carnival Corporation Ltd. shares during the May 2026 dual-listed-company unification. |
| **Second Measure / Earnest Analytics** | Independent consumer-transaction-data research firms that estimate market share for consumer platforms (e.g. US food-delivery apps) from aggregated card-transaction panels — a third-party (non-company-sourced) citation basis for Moat Signal "market share" evidence in this framework, analogous to Dell'Oro Group's role for telecom-equipment share data. Cited for DoorDash's US food-delivery share (~60.7% end of 2024, ~67% by 2025-2026, vs. Uber Eats ~23%). |
| **Securities class action / shareholder-rights investigation** | A law firm's public solicitation of shareholders to join a potential lawsuit alleging a company issued false/misleading statements or omitted material information (distinct from a regulator-initiated probe like an SEC or DOJ investigation). An open, unresolved allegation — like a going-concern/accounting-integrity allegation — that should be tracked as a risk flag, never treated as a settled finding of wrongdoing until a court or regulator actually rules on it. Doximity (DOCS) faced multiple such investigations (Schall Law Firm, Pomerantz LLP, Halper Sadeh) opened in mid-2026 centered on whether management adequately disclosed AI-cost margin pressure ahead of its 13 May 2026 guidance miss. |
| **Sovereign AI** | National/government-funded AI computing infrastructure (e.g. in Saudi Arabia, Japan, France) built to keep a country's AI workloads and data within its own borders rather than relying on foreign hyperscalers — an emerging demand category for AI-chip vendors like NVIDIA, distinct from traditional hyperscaler/enterprise demand, cited as Growth sub-score TAM-expansion evidence in this framework's 2026-07-05 NVDA session. |
| **S-1 / S-1/A (Registration Statement)** | The SEC form a company files to register securities for a public offering (IPO); an "S-1/A" is an amendment to it. This is the primary Rule-0-compliant fundamentals source for a recently-IPO'd company (e.g. SpaceX/SPCX in this framework's 2026-07-13 session) that has not yet filed its first 10-K or 10-Q, containing PCAOB-audited historical annual financials and any unaudited interim-period statements included in the filing. |
| **de-SPAC merger** | The transaction in which a SPAC completes its combination with a private operating company, converting the private company into a publicly-traded one under the SPAC's listing. Oklo Inc. (OKLO) went public this way in May 2024 (AltC Acquisition Corp. → Oklo Inc.), cited in this framework's 2026-07-16 OKLO session as the reason its FY2022/FY2023 financials don't reconcile cleanly across data vendors. *(New term.)* |
| **SPAC** | Special Purpose Acquisition Company — a shell company that raises money via its own IPO for the specific purpose of merging with a private company to take it public, as an alternative to a traditional IPO. Often leaves the resulting company with a short, distorted public earnings history (one-off preferred-share/warrant accounting charges, thin trailing financials) that complicates standard quality/valuation checks. |
| **Spin-off** | A corporate transaction where a company separates part of its business into a new, independently-traded public company, typically by distributing shares of the new entity to existing shareholders pro rata. Creates a data-integrity trap for trailing financial-statement analysis: post-spinoff live price and share count reflect the smaller remaining company, while trailing income-statement/balance-sheet/cash-flow figures (until enough post-spinoff quarters accumulate) still reflect the larger pre-spinoff combined entity — ratios mixing the two are invalid until restated. FedEx's 1 June 2026 spin-off of FedEx Freight (FDXF) is an example tracked in this framework. |
| **Stablecoin** | A cryptocurrency token designed to hold a stable value, typically pegged 1:1 to a fiat currency like the US dollar. Relevant to card networks like Visa both as a potential competitive disintermediation threat (crypto-native settlement rails bypassing the traditional card network) and as a new service opportunity — Visa has launched its own USDC stablecoin settlement offering for issuer/acquirer partners, cited in this framework's 2026-07-05 V session. |
| **SIDF (Saudi Industrial Development Fund)** | A Saudi government development-finance institution that provides subsidized industrial loans. Lucid Group's (LCID) ~$1.4B SIDF loan facility (entered 2022) is one of several credit lines cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 LCID session, alongside its ABL Credit Facility and its DDTL Credit Facility with controlling shareholder Ayar. *(New term.)* |
| **Standstill agreement** | A temporary agreement between a financially distressed borrower and its lenders in which the lenders agree not to demand repayment, call a default, or enforce other remedies while a longer-term restructuring is negotiated. A signal of active, unresolved balance-sheet distress — not a resolved situation — until a definitive restructuring plan is signed. |
| **Star Rating (CMS Star Rating)** | CMS's annual 1-to-5-star quality score for each Medicare Advantage plan (based on clinical-quality, member-experience, and operational measures), published each October for the following payment year, which affects both a plan's CMS bonus payments and its competitiveness in the annual enrollment period. UnitedHealthcare guided that roughly 78% of its Medicare Advantage membership would be in 4-star-or-above plans for 2026, cited in this framework's 2026-07-13 UNH session. |
| **Strategic Customer Agreement (SCA)** | A binding, multi-year take-or-pay supply contract in which a customer commits to purchase a set volume at a pre-agreed (often floor) price, typically backed by a cash deposit. Micron disclosed 16 SCAs at its Q3 FY2026 earnings call (24 Jun 2026) covering ~20% of DRAM and ~30% of NAND volume, ~$100B in floor-price contracted revenue, and ~$22B in customer cash deposits/commitments — cited in this framework's 2026-07-10 MU Quality Score addendum as Growth (TAM/pricing-power) and Moat (brand premium) modifier evidence. |
| **SSD (Solid-State Drive)** | A flash-memory-based storage device (built from NAND chips) used in computers/servers as the modern replacement for spinning hard drives — the end product much of a memory maker's NAND output is sold into. |
| **TAM** | Total Addressable Market — the total revenue opportunity available if a company captured 100% of its target market. |
| **T-Bills (Treasury Bills)** | Short-maturity (≤1 year) US government debt securities — effectively cash-equivalent in credit/liquidity risk. Berkshire Hathaway held $339.3B of these at 2026-03-31, on top of $51.5B in cash and equivalents — a combined liquidity position roughly 7× its total debt, cited in this framework's 2026-07-15 BRK session as a major source of ambiguity in computing Enterprise Value for the company. *(New term.)* |
| **TBVPS (Tangible Book Value Per Share)** | Tangible book value (book value minus intangible assets) divided by shares outstanding — the per-share input to a P/TBV multiple. |
| **TCV (Total Contract Value)** | The full dollar value of a signed customer contract over its entire term (distinct from ACV, which annualizes it, and ARR, which measures currently-recognized run-rate). Palantir's Q1 FY2026 TCV closed was $2.41B, +61% YoY — cited as growth-momentum evidence in this framework's 2026-07-10 PLTR Quality Score addendum. |
| **TCO (Total Cost of Ownership)** | The full cost of using a piece of hardware over its life (purchase price plus power, cooling, networking, and software/support costs), not just the sticker price — the standard basis on which competing AI chips (e.g. NVIDIA vs. AMD) are compared, since a cheaper chip can still lose on TCO if it needs more power or units to do the same job. |
| **Take-or-pay** | A supply-contract structure where the buyer must pay for a committed volume of product whether or not it actually takes delivery — shifts demand risk from the seller to the buyer and gives the seller (here, Micron under its Strategic Customer Agreements) unusually high revenue/margin visibility for a historically cyclical, spot-price-driven commodity business. |
| **Terminal Value** | In a multi-stage DCF, the lump-sum value assigned to all cash flows beyond the explicit forecast period (e.g., after Year 10), estimated via a perpetuity-growth formula and then discounted back to today — often the single largest component of a DCF's total value, which is why Rule 4's sanity check flags a model where it exceeds 75% of total value. |
| **Total liquidity (vs. cash and cash equivalents)** | A company's own broader, self-disclosed liquidity figure — typically cash and cash equivalents plus short/long-term investments plus undrawn committed credit-facility capacity — distinct from (and larger than) the plain "cash and cash equivalents" balance-sheet line. Lucid Group (LCID) disclosed ~$3.2B of "total liquidity" as of Q1 2026 vs. $714M of cash, equivalents, and investments narrowly defined, and cited the broader figure in its 14 July 2026 statement denying bankruptcy rumors — a real distinction (undrawn credit lines are genuinely available), but one that overstates near-term spendable cash if read uncritically, since it includes capacity the company would still need to draw down (and its lender, in Lucid's case its own controlling shareholder, would still need to fund). Cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 LCID session. *(New term.)* |
| **Toyota Production System (TPS)** | Toyota's proprietary lean-manufacturing methodology (just-in-time production, jidoka/built-in-quality, continuous improvement) — widely credited as the origin of "lean manufacturing" globally and a genuine, qualitatively well-documented source of Toyota's cost discipline, though not credited as a Moat Signal "scale cost advantage" in this framework's 2026-07-12 TM session for lack of a specific cost-per-unit citation vs. smaller competitors. |
| **TIC (Testing, Inspection & Certification)** | An asset-light services industry (Bureau Veritas, SGS, Intertek are the major global players) where inspectors/engineers verify that products, facilities, or systems meet safety, quality, or regulatory standards — labor-heavy, so personnel costs sit directly in cost of sales and mechanically compress reported gross margin well below a typical product-company's, even when returns on capital (ROIC) and cash generation are strong. Flagged in this framework's 2026-07-18 EU screening session as a possible case for adapting the standard >40% gross-margin Phase 01 filter to labor-based service models. *(New term.)* |
| **Tri-merge (credit report)** | A mortgage-industry practice of pulling and merging credit reports/scores from all three national bureaus (Experian, Equifax, TransUnion) into a single combined report for a lending decision, typically handled by specialist reseller firms — the distribution channel FICO's 2025 direct-license program lets lenders partially bypass. |
| **Treasury yield (10Y)** | The interest rate the US government pays on its 10-year bonds — the standard "risk-free rate" benchmark used throughout this framework's Rate Environment Gate. |
| **TrendForce** | An independent Taiwan-based market-research firm covering the semiconductor and memory (DRAM/NAND) industries; its quarterly supplier-revenue and market-share reports are a third-party (non-company-sourced) citation basis for Moat Signal "market share" evidence in this framework, e.g. SanDisk's (SNDK) NAND flash market share moving from 12.4% (Q3 2025, TrendForce) to 13.9% tied-for-4th (Q1 2026, TrendForce) in the 2026-07-15 SNDK session. *(New term.)* |
| **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** | The most recent 12 months of reported financial results, as opposed to a fiscal-year or forward-looking figure. |
| **TTS (Treasury and Trade Solutions)** | Citigroup's cash-management and trade-finance business line for corporate/institutional clients (part of its Services segment) — cited as Moat Signal evidence (market share gains, switching-cost/network-effect infrastructure) in this framework's 2026-07-12 C session. |
| **Uncertain tax position release** | A one-off GAAP accounting event where a company reverses a previously-reserved tax liability (an "uncertain tax position," held in reserve because the company wasn't sure a tax authority would ultimately agree with its filing position) after the tax authority resolves the matter in the company's favor — e.g. AMD's FY2025 ~$853M benefit from an IRS "dual consolidated losses" reasonable-cause relief determination, approved April 2025. Like a deferred tax valuation allowance release, it produces an artificially low (or negative) effective tax rate and inflated net income/EPS in the recognition period and shouldn't be treated as a sustainable tax rate — normalize using a clean, non-distorted period's effective tax rate instead (e.g. AMD's adjacent clean quarters at ~14.9%) for NOPAT/ROIC and other profitability-quality calculations. See the related **Deferred tax valuation allowance release** entry above for the sibling mechanism. |
| **Up-C structure** | An "umbrella partnership corporation" structure, common among companies with a controlling pre-IPO/private equity sponsor, where the public company (the entity whose ticker trades on an exchange) holds an economic interest in an operating partnership alongside other unit-holders (e.g. a founder, sponsor, or strategic partner), rather than owning 100% of the operating business directly. Flagged in this framework as a data-integrity trap: different data vendors can report meaningfully different "shares outstanding" figures for the same company depending on whether they count only the publicly-traded share class or also the economically-equivalent partnership/holding units — caught in CHTR's 2026-06-29 session as an unresolved, unconfirmed hypothesis for a share-count discrepancy across vendors, not an independently verified fact. |
| **Value trap** | A stock that looks statistically cheap (low valuation score/multiples) but stays cheap or keeps falling because the underlying business quality is actually deteriorating or was never strong enough to support a re-rating — the risk this framework's 80.0+ Quality Score gate and Composite Score are specifically designed to surface, rather than acting on an attractively-cheap valuation score alone. |
| **VAS (Value-Added Services)** | Visa's non-core-network product line — fraud/risk tools, tokenization, authentication, advisory, and issuing/acceptance support — sold on top of its core card-network fees. Visa's fastest-growing revenue segment (28% YoY growth, ~30% of net revenue in fiscal Q2 2026), cited as Growth sub-score TAM-expansion evidence in this framework's 2026-07-05 V session. |
| **Volatility trading halt (circuit breaker)** | An automatic, temporary (typically 5-minute) pause in trading a US-listed stock, triggered by an exchange's regulatory circuit-breaker rules when its price moves beyond a set threshold in a short window — intended to let information disseminate and order books rebalance before trading resumes, not itself a judgment about the underlying news. Lucid Group (LCID) shares triggered two such halts on 14 July 2026 amid a bankruptcy-rumor-driven plunge of as much as ~40%+ intraday, cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 LCID session. *(New term.)* |
| **Wafer** | The thin silicon disc on which semiconductor chips are manufactured — the physical unit a foundry sells its manufacturing capacity by, cited in this framework's 2026-07-12 TSM session. |
| **WACC** | Weighted Average Cost of Capital — the blended cost a company pays for its debt and equity financing; used as the discount rate in a DCF. |
| **Wallet share** | An investment bank's share of total global fee revenue paid across an industry category (e.g. investment banking fees) in a given period — a market-share metric specific to fee-based advisory/underwriting businesses. JPMorgan ranked #1 globally in investment banking fees with an 8.4% wallet share in FY2025, cited in this framework's 2026-07-14 JPM session. |
| **Warrant (fair-value adjustment)** | A security giving its holder the right to buy stock at a preset price, commonly issued to SPAC sponsors/investors as a sweetener. When GAAP requires a warrant to be classified as a liability rather than equity (common in SPAC-era capital structures), it must be marked to fair value every reporting period, and the resulting gain/loss (larger when the stock is more volatile) runs through the income statement as a non-cash item unrelated to operating performance. Trump Media & Technology Group's (DJT) FY2024 results included a $225.9M non-cash `FairValueAdjustmentOfWarrants` loss, a legacy of its March 2024 de-SPAC merger structure — cited in this framework's 2026-07-17 DJT session as one reason GAAP net income has run far more negative than actual cash burn (FCF) in some periods. *(New term.)* |
| **xAI Merger** | SpaceX's acquisition of X.AI Holdings Corp. (Elon Musk's AI company, itself the post-merger parent of X/Twitter following the March 2025 "X Merger"), effective 2 February 2026 — combined SpaceX's space/satellite business with xAI's Grok AI models, X's social/advertising platform, and AI compute infrastructure (the Colossus data centers) into the single consolidated public company evaluated in this framework's 2026-07-13 SPCX session. See also Common-control accounting above. |
| **XBRL (eXtensible Business Reporting Language)** | A structured, machine-readable data format the SEC requires public companies to tag their financial-statement figures in — lets this framework pull precise line-item figures (revenue, net income, debt, etc.) directly from a company's own filings via the SEC's `companyfacts`/`companyconcept` APIs, rather than reading raw filing text or waiting on a third-party data vendor. |
| **ΔNWC (Change in Net Working Capital)** | The period-over-period change in current assets minus current liabilities — a deduction in unlevered FCF calculations. |

## Behavioral-finance & speculation terms

These came up in the 2026-07-01 discussion of a possible speculation module bolted onto this framework's core long-term rules — not yet an adopted part of the framework itself.

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Barbell strategy** | A portfolio shape (popularized by Nassim Taleb) that pairs a very large, very safe allocation with a small, high-risk/high-convexity allocation, deliberately skipping the "medium risk" middle. |
| **Core-satellite (portfolio strategy)** | Splitting a portfolio into a large "core" of long-term strategic holdings (typically 70–90%) and a smaller "satellite" sleeve (10–30%) for tactical or opportunistic bets — standard practice among pensions, endowments, and wealth managers since the 1980s. |
| **Disposition effect** | The behavioral bias of selling winning positions too early (to lock in a good feeling) while holding losing positions too long (to avoid realizing the loss) — the opposite of a pre-committed, rules-based exit. |
| **Expectancy** | (Average win × win rate) − (Average loss × loss rate) — the expected profit or loss per trade across many trades. A strategy needs positive expectancy after costs to be worth running, independent of how often any single trade wins. |
| **House money effect** | The tendency to take bigger risks with money that feels like "winnings" than with money treated as core capital — the same mental-accounting mechanism that a deliberately separate speculation account can exploit in the investor's favor, or that can backfire as escalating bets on the theory that it's "not really your money." |
| **Mad money account** | Benjamin Graham's term (*The Intelligent Investor*) for a small, strictly separate sub-account set aside for speculation, capped at roughly 10% of total wealth, with a hard rule against ever mixing its funds or its mindset with the core investment portfolio. |
| **Mental accounting** | Richard Thaler's term for the tendency to treat money differently depending on which mental "bucket" it's assigned to, rather than as fully fungible. |
| **Time-stop** | A pre-set exit rule that closes a position after a fixed amount of time regardless of price action (e.g. "exit in 90 days if the catalyst hasn't played out"), distinct from a price-based stop-loss and typically paired with one in short-horizon trades. |
| **Turnover (portfolio turnover)** | The percentage of a portfolio's holdings bought/sold over a given period (typically a year) — a proxy for trading frequency; higher turnover mechanically raises transaction costs and, in taxable accounts, short-term tax drag. |

## Technical-analysis & speculation-strategy terms

These support the strategy-selection layer in [speculation-strategies.md](speculation-strategies.md) — used only inside the speculation sleeve, never in core Phase 01/02 scoring.

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **ADX (Average Directional Index)** | A 0–100 indicator (Wilder) measuring how strongly a stock is trending, regardless of direction — used in this framework to decide whether a momentum/breakout strategy or a mean-reversion strategy applies (ADX ≥25 = trending, <20 = range-bound). |
| **ATR (Average True Range)** | A Wilder-smoothed average of a stock's daily trading range — a volatility measure in price terms, used in this framework to set a name-specific stop-loss distance rather than a generic percentage. |
| **Bollinger Bands** | A band drawn a fixed number of standard deviations (typically 2) above and below a moving average (typically 20-day) — used in this framework to flag when a price move looks statistically stretched, as one input to a mean-reversion bounce thesis. |
| **Donchian Channel** | The highest high and lowest low over a lookback window (typically 20 days) — the breakout trigger from the "Turtle Trading" system, used in this framework to time momentum entries and to set technically meaningful (not arbitrary) price targets. |
| **Momentum factor** | The academically documented tendency (Jegadeesh & Titman, 1993) for stocks that have risen over the past 3–12 months to keep outperforming over the following months, and vice versa — the evidence basis for this framework's momentum/breakout speculation strategy. |
| **Moving average (SMA / EMA)** | The average of a stock's closing price over a fixed number of past days — Simple (SMA, equal-weighted) or Exponential (EMA, more weight on recent days). Used in this framework to classify trend direction (e.g. price above/below its 50-day and 200-day averages). |
| **PEAD (Post-Earnings Announcement Drift)** | An academically documented anomaly (Bernard & Thomas, 1989) where a stock continues drifting in the direction of an earnings surprise for weeks afterward, rather than instantly repricing — the evidence basis for this framework's post-earnings speculation strategy. |
| **RSI (Relative Strength Index)** | A 0–100 momentum oscillator (Wilder) measuring the size of recent gains versus losses — readings ≤30 are conventionally read as "oversold" and ≥70 as "overbought," one input to this framework's mean-reversion bounce strategy. |
| **RVOL (Relative Volume)** | Today's trading volume divided by its recent (typically 20-day) average — used in this framework to confirm whether a breakout has real participation behind it (high RVOL) or is likely to fail (low RVOL). |
| **Support / resistance** | A price level where a stock has historically tended to stop falling (support) or stop rising (resistance), used in this framework (via the Donchian channel and 52-week high/low) to set technically meaningful entry, target, and stop levels instead of arbitrary round numbers. |

## Framework-specific terms

These are this repository's own vocabulary — defined in [strategy.md](strategy.md) and [valuation-scoring.md](valuation-scoring.md), not standard Wall Street terms.

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Catalyst window** | The timeframe (per Rule 10, typically 18–24 months) within which a documented, specific event is expected to close the gap between price and fair value — required before the Upside/Downside Modifier can credit large expected upside. |
| **Composite Score** | This framework's single ranking number (0.0–100.0, 0.0 = most attractive) blending the Quality Score and the Valuation Score 50/50 — `0.50 × (100 − Quality Score) + 0.50 × Valuation Score` — computed only for companies that have already cleared the 80.0+ Quality Score gate. Used against the Phase 03/05 action tables instead of the raw valuation score. See [valuation-scoring.md](valuation-scoring.md). |
| **Conglomerate rule** | This framework's instruction ([strategy.md](strategy.md)) to consolidate a captive financial subsidiary's debt into the Net Debt/EBITDA ratio rather than carving it out favorably — e.g. Toyota Financial Services' captive vehicle-loan/lease debt for Toyota, or Stellantis Financial Services' debt for Stellantis. Applied by extension to capital spending (not just debt) in this framework's 2026-07-12 TM session, where it was the central driver of the Balance Sheet and FCF Quality sub-score findings. |
| **Debt Gate** | This framework's balance-sheet check on Net Debt/EBITDA (Hybrid Upgrade 5) — standard threshold <2.5×, relaxed to <4× for asset-light payment networks/exchanges with strong interest coverage and investment-grade ratings. |
| **Fallen Angel** | This framework's term for a previously-qualified, formerly high-quality company that has stumbled — evaluated via the Turnaround Sub-Gate (Hybrid Upgrade 4) rather than the standard screen. |
| **Hard disqualifier** | One of three Quality Score conditions (FCF/NI conversion <70% for 2+ consecutive years without a documented growth-capex explanation; Net Debt/EBITDA over the Debt Gate threshold; not FCF-positive for 3+ consecutive years) that fails a company regardless of its weighted Quality Score — see [quality-scoring.md](quality-scoring.md). Not every hard disqualifier carries a carve-out: the FCF-positivity check has none, while the FCF/NI conversion check can be waived with cited evidence of growth-driven (not maintenance) capex. |
| **Human Override** | A position opened or held outside the framework's own rules — e.g. bought at a valuation score of 50.0+ (Watchlist/Expensive) or with a quality criterion waived. Tracked for life in [override-log.md](../portfolio/override-log.md), separate from the unrelated *Structural Quality Override* term below. |
| **Hybrid Upgrade** | One of 7 framework-specific rule additions layered on top of the base 6-phase strategy (e.g. Owner Earnings, Historical PE Modifier, PEG sub-score) — see [strategy.md](strategy.md). |
| **Phase 01–06** | The six sequential stages of this framework: Universe Screening → Valuation Scoring → Entry/Position Sizing → Continuous Monitoring → Dynamic Trimming → Full Exit. |
| **PW (Probability-Weighted) Fair Value** | This framework's blended fair value estimate — 25% bull case + 50% base case + 25% bear case (Rule 7) — used as the single fair-value input to both the order setup and the Upside/Downside Modifier. |
| **Qualified Quality List** | The output of Phase 01 screening — the set of companies that passed the quality gate and are eligible for valuation scoring. |
| **Quality Score** | This framework's 0.0–100.0 continuous score (0.0 = lowest quality, 100.0 = highest) grading the Phase 01 criteria (profitability, margins, growth, balance sheet, moat signal, FCF quality) instead of treating them as simple pass/fail. A company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02 valuation scoring at all. See [quality-scoring.md](quality-scoring.md). |
| **Rate Environment Gate** | The mandatory pre-check run before every Phase 02 valuation score, comparing Earnings Yield against the 10-Year Treasury yield and applying a Rate Regime Modifier. |
| **Rate Regime Modifier** | An additive adjustment (−10 to +10) applied to the valuation score based on which Treasury-yield bracket the market is currently in. |
| **Rule 0** | This framework's standing instruction to always fetch a live, current price before any valuation work — never infer price from multiples or stale data. |
| **Rule 9** | This framework's list of fundamental events that force an immediate re-valuation regardless of schedule: quarterly earnings, a guidance revision, a management change, material M&A, a macro shift, or a >15% stock-price move with no identified cause. |
| **Structural Quality Override** | A check that suspends the Historical PE Modifier's "expensive" penalty when a stock's higher multiple is justified by a genuine improvement in the business (margin expansion, ROIC improvement) rather than mere euphoria. |
| **Turnaround Sub-Gate** | The conditional path (Hybrid Upgrade 4) that lets a company failing some quality criteria still enter as a small (2–3%) position if it passes 5 specific tests (historical ROIC, insider buying, margin of safety, debt level, identifiable moat). |
| **Upside/Downside Modifier (Expected-Return Modifier)** | An additive ±15 adjustment to the valuation score based on expected annual return (the gap to PW Fair Value, annualized over the catalyst window, plus intrinsic growth and shareholder yield) — folds the forward-looking dimension into the score so strong expected upside pulls a name toward "buy" and a thin/negative expected return pushes it toward "trim/sell." |
| **Valuation score** | This framework's 0.0–100.0 continuous score (0.0 = cheapest, 100.0 = most expensive) combining the Phase 02 valuation sub-scores, the Rate Environment Gate, and the Upside/Downside Modifier into a single number that maps to an action band (Very Cheap/Cheap/Fair Value/Expensive) — see [valuation-scoring.md](valuation-scoring.md). |
| **Watchlist (action band)** | The framework's action recommendation for a valuation score of 50.0–69.9 — the stock is fairly-to-fully valued and the rule is "no new entry," distinct from the repo's `watchlist/` directory (which tracks *every* scored ticker, held or not). |
