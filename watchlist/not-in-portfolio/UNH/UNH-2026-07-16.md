## Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **CMS (Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services)** | The US federal agency administering Medicare, Medicaid, and Medicare Advantage, including setting payment rates, the risk-adjustment methodology, and Star Ratings. |
| **DOJ (Department of Justice)** | UNH is under open, unresolved criminal and civil DOJ investigation into Medicare Advantage billing/risk-adjustment practices — a risk flag, not a settled finding. |
| **FCF / FCF Yield / FCF/NI conversion ratio** | Free Cash Flow; FCF ÷ Market Cap; FCF ÷ Net Income (a cash-quality check) — all comfortably passed this session. |
| **Hard disqualifier** | A Quality Score condition that fails a company regardless of weighted score; none fired for UNH — it failed on weighted score alone. |
| **Managed care** | A health-insurance model (Medicare Advantage, Medicaid managed care, employer plans) where a private insurer administers care/payment for a government program or employer in exchange for a fixed or risk-adjusted fee. |
| **MCR (Medical Care Ratio) / MLR (Medical Loss Ratio)** | The percentage of premium revenue paid out in medical claims; UNH's swung from 71.1% (Q2 2025) to 89.9% (Q3 2025) before improving to 83.9% (Q1 2026). |
| **Medicare Advantage (MA)** | A privately-administered alternative to traditional Medicare; UNH's largest product line and the center of its 2025 margin crisis and DOJ investigation. |
| **Net Debt/EBITDA** | Net debt divided by EBITDA — a leverage ratio; this framework's primary balance-sheet-risk gate. UNH's is 2.155×, under the 2.5× threshold. |
| **Quality Score** | This framework's 0.0–100.0 continuous score (higher = better); 80.0+ required to proceed to valuation scoring. UNH scored 36.1. |
| **ROIC** | Return on Invested Capital — how efficiently a company turns invested capital into profit; a core quality signal in this framework. |
| **Rule 0** | This framework's standing instruction to always fetch a live, current price before any valuation work. |
| **Rule 9** | This framework's list of fundamental events that force an immediate re-valuation: earnings, guidance revisions, management changes, M&A, macro shifts, or a >15% unexplained price move. |
