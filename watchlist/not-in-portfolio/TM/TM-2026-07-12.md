## Glossary

See [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md) for the standing definitions file. Terms used in this entry:

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **ADR / ADS (American Depositary Receipt / Share)** | A US-exchange-listed security representing shares of a non-US company; Toyota's is a conventional sponsored ADR, 1 ADS = 10 ordinary shares. |
| **Conglomerate rule** | This framework's instruction to consolidate a captive financial subsidiary's debt (and, per this session's extension, its capital spending) into the relevant ratios rather than carving it out favorably — the central driver of Toyota's Balance Sheet and FCF Quality findings. |
| **Equipment on operating leases** | Vehicles placed into a company's own leasing programs; a distinct, SEC-disclosed capital-spending line for automakers with large captive-leasing arms, central to this session's CapEx/FCF judgment call. |
| **Hard disqualifier** | A Quality Score condition that fails a company regardless of weighted score; two independently fire for Toyota (Net Debt/EBITDA; FCF/NI conversion). |
| **Kelley Blue Book (KBB) Best Resale Value Award** | A US automotive-industry award/study recognizing the highest projected 5-year resale value by brand; cited as Toyota's/Lexus's brand-premium Moat Signal evidence. |
| **Moat** | A durable competitive advantage protecting a business's profits — Toyota scored 40.0 (2 of 5 signals: market share, brand premium). |
| **Net Debt/EBITDA** | This framework's primary balance-sheet-risk gate; Toyota's is 4.96×, over both the standard 2.5× and asset-light-override 4× thresholds. |
| **Quality Score** | This framework's 0.0–100.0 continuous quality grade; 80.0+ required to proceed to valuation scoring. Toyota scored 24.7. |
| **ROIC** | Return on Invested Capital; Toyota's is 4.07%, depressed by its captive-finance segment's leverage structure. |
| **Toyota Production System (TPS)** | Toyota's lean-manufacturing methodology; qualitatively well documented but not credited as a Moat Signal this session for lack of a specific cost-per-unit citation. |
