## Glossary

See [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md) for the standing definitions file. Terms used in this addendum (beyond those already covered by the 06-14 session log's glossary):

- **CFR (Corporate Family Rating)** — Moody's rating of a company's overall corporate family (consolidated) credit risk, distinct from ratings on specific individual debt instruments; FICO's CFR is Ba2, sub-investment grade.
- **CID (Civil Investigative Demand)** — a formal, subpoena-like investigative tool used by a state Attorney General (or the FTC) to compel a company to produce documents and information *before* any lawsuit is filed — an investigation-stage step, not itself an enforcement action or finding of wrongdoing. Florida's AG issued one to FICO around 2 Jul 2026 as part of a state antitrust investigation; FICO's response is due 5 Aug 2026.
- **GSE (Government-Sponsored Enterprise)** — a federally-chartered entity (here, Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac) that buys and securitizes conforming mortgage loans; their acceptance criteria (which have long required the FICO Score specifically) are a key source of FICO's mortgage-market moat.
- **Hard disqualifier** — see [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md); FICO's Net Debt/EBITDA (2.61×) fires this one independently of the 79.4–79.6 weighted score.
- **Invested Capital** — see [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md); computing it the framework's standard way (Debt + Equity − Cash) is itself distorted for FICO by the same negative-equity issue that breaks conventional ROIC, per the flagged judgment-call note above.
- **NOPAT** — Net Operating Profit After Tax, the numerator of ROIC.
- **Quality Score** — this framework's 0-100.0 continuous quality grade; a company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02. FICO scores 79.4-79.6 on its first-ever computation — the closest-to-passing score seen under this engine so far.
- **ROIC** — Return on Invested Capital; undefined via the conventional NI/Equity route for FICO due to negative GAAP equity, handled here via a capped-score judgment call (see above).
- **Rule 0** — this framework's standing instruction to always fetch a live, current price before any valuation work.
- **Rule 9** — this framework's list of events that force an immediate re-valuation: earnings, guidance revision, management change, M&A, macro shift, or a >15% unexplained price move. None fired this session.
- **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** — the most recent 12 months of reported results.
