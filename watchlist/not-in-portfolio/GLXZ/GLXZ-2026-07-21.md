## Glossary

See [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md) for the standing definitions file. Terms used above:

- **CAGR** — Compound Annual Growth Rate.
- **EBITDA** — Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation & Amortization — a rough proxy for cash operating profit.
- **FCF/NI conversion ratio** — Free Cash Flow ÷ Net Income, an earnings-quality check.
- **Hard disqualifier** — one of three Quality Score conditions that fails a company regardless of its weighted score. GLXZ fails on Net Debt/EBITDA.
- **Moat** — a durable competitive advantage protecting a business's profits from competitors.
- **Net Debt/EBITDA** — a leverage ratio (net debt ÷ EBITDA) measuring how many years of cash operating profit it would take to pay off all debt; this framework's primary balance-sheet-risk gate.
- **Outside Date** — the contractual deadline in an M&A agreement by which the deal must close or either party may walk away.
- **OTC Pink Sheets** — the lowest, most lightly-regulated OTC quotation tier; thin/no-quote trading is common even for a fully SEC-reporting company.
- **Quality Score** — this framework's 0–100.0 continuous quality grade; a company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02. GLXZ scores 53.0.
- **Rule 0** — always fetch a live price (and primary financial data) before any valuation work — never infer or invent it.
- **Rule 9** — the framework's list of events that force an immediate re-valuation: earnings, guidance revision, management change, M&A, macro shift, or a >15% unexplained price move.
- **Stockholders' Deficit** — negative Total Stockholders' Equity, when Total Liabilities exceed Total Assets.
- **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** — the most recent 12 months of reported financial results.
