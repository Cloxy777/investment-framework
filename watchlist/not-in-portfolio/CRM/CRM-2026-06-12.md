## Glossary

See [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md) for the standing definitions file. Terms used in this addendum:

- **8-K** — a US company's "current report" filed with the SEC disclosing a material event between regular filings; used here as the primary source for the Q1 FY2027 earnings release (filed 2026-05-27).
- **10-K / 10-Q** — a US public company's annual / quarterly report filed with the SEC, containing audited/unaudited financial statements; used here as the primary source for CRM's balance sheet (as of 2026-04-30) and business/competition disclosures.
- **Agentforce** — Salesforce's suite of autonomous AI agent products; its Annual Recurring Revenue (ARR) is cited here as Growth sub-score TAM-expansion evidence.
- **ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue)** — the annualized value of active subscription contracts; a forward-looking demand metric distinct from GAAP reported revenue.
- **ASR (Accelerated Share Repurchase)** — a buyback structure where a company receives most shares upfront from a bank in exchange for a large upfront payment, later trued up based on the stock's average price; CRM's $25B ASR, partly funded by the March 2026 senior-notes issuance discussed in this addendum.
- **BalanceSheet_Score** — this framework's 0-100 Quality Score sub-score derived from Net Debt/EBITDA.
- **CAGR** — Compound Annual Growth Rate.
- **cRPO (current Remaining Performance Obligation)** — the portion of RPO expected to be recognized as revenue within 12 months; a nearer-term demand signal than total RPO.
- **EBIT / EBITDA** — Earnings Before Interest and Taxes / before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization.
- **Effective tax rate** — the actual share of pretax income paid as tax; used here to derive NOPAT from EBIT.
- **FCF/NI conversion ratio** — Free Cash Flow ÷ Net Income, an earnings-quality check.
- **GAAP** — Generally Accepted Accounting Principles.
- **Gross Margin** — Gross Profit ÷ Revenue.
- **Hard disqualifier** — one of three Quality Score conditions that fails a company regardless of its weighted score; none fire for CRM this session.
- **Invested Capital** — the capital (debt + equity, this framework's glossary says netted for cash) deployed in a business; the ROIC denominator. This session applies the same methodology flag the CIEN session raised: an internal inconsistency between the glossary's cash-netted definition and Yahoo's own raw `InvestedCapital` field.
- **Moat** — a durable competitive advantage.
- **NOPAT** — Net Operating Profit After Tax, the numerator of ROIC.
- **Net Debt/EBITDA** — a leverage ratio; this framework's primary balance-sheet-risk gate.
- **Quality Score** — this framework's 0-100.0 continuous quality grade; a company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02. CRM scores 62.3 on its first-ever computation.
- **ROIC** — Return on Invested Capital.
- **RPO (Remaining Performance Obligation)** — total contracted revenue not yet recognized, including amounts beyond the next 12 months.
- **Rule 0** — this framework's standing instruction to always fetch a live, current price before any valuation work.
- **Rule 9** — this framework's list of events that force an immediate re-valuation: earnings, guidance revision, management change, M&A, macro shift, or a >15% unexplained price move.
- **Senior Notes** — unsecured corporate bonds ranking above subordinated debt; CRM issued roughly $25B across new tranches (2028-2066 maturities) in March 2026 to help fund its ASR.
- **TAM (Total Addressable Market)** — the total revenue opportunity available if a company captured its entire target market.
- **Turnaround Sub-Gate** — an alternate Phase 01 path (Upgrade 4) requiring historical ROIC >15% for 5+ of the past 10 years plus insider buying, an independent fair-value margin-of-safety check, low leverage, and an identifiable moat; already closed for CRM per the 06-12 session (condition 1 fails outright).
- **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** — the most recent 12 months of reported financial results.
- **WACC (Weighted Average Cost of Capital)** — the blended return a company must earn on its capital to create value; CRM's ROIC has historically sat below its own WACC.
