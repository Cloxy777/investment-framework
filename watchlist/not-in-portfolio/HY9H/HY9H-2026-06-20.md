## Glossary

See [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md) for the standing definitions file. Terms used in this addendum (all already defined there):

- **ADR / ADS (American Depositary Receipt / Share)** — SKHY, the new Nasdaq-listed instrument (1 ordinary share : 10 ADRs), distinct from HY9H's existing 1:1 Frankfurt GDR.
- **BalanceSheet_Score** — this framework's 0-100 Quality Score sub-score derived from Net Debt/EBITDA.
- **CAGR** — Compound Annual Growth Rate.
- **Capital rotation** — investors shifting capital between assets/sectors; the glossary's own entry cross-references this exact SKHY listing as a candidate driver of MU's early-July pullback.
- **Dilutive (capital raise)** — issuing new shares, shrinking existing holders' ownership percentage; the ~177.9M new SKHY ADRs (~17.79M ordinary-share equivalent, ~2.5% of shares outstanding) are a modest example.
- **FCF/NI conversion ratio** — Free Cash Flow ÷ Net Income, an earnings-quality check; HY9H's TTM ratio is 54.16%.
- **GDR (Global Depositary Receipt)** — the HY9H instrument type; distinct from an ADR (US-specific).
- **HBM (High-Bandwidth Memory)** — the premium stacked-DRAM format for AI accelerators; SK hynix's core growth/moat driver.
- **Hard disqualifier** — one of three Quality Score conditions that fails a company regardless of its weighted score; HY9H's FCF-positivity check fires this one, with no carve-out available (unlike the FCF/NI-conversion check, which is carved out here on documented growth-capex grounds).
- **IPO (Initial Public Offering)** — SK hynix's $28-29B Nasdaq ADR offering, listing 2026-07-10.
- **Moat** — a durable competitive advantage.
- **Net Debt/EBITDA** — a leverage ratio; this framework's primary balance-sheet-risk gate.
- **Quality Score** — this framework's 0-100.0 continuous quality grade; a company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02. HY9H scores 66.2.
- **ROIC** — Return on Invested Capital.
- **Rule 0** — always fetch a live price before any valuation work.
- **Rule 9** — the framework's list of events that force an immediate re-valuation: earnings, guidance revision, management change, M&A, macro shift, or a >15% unexplained price move.
- **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** — the most recent 12 months of reported results.
