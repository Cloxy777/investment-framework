## Glossary

See [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md) for the standing definitions file. Terms used below (beyond those already covered by the 06-22 session's glossary above):

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **10b5-1 Trading Plan** | A pre-arranged SEC trading plan letting an insider schedule stock sales/purchases in advance as an affirmative defense against insider-trading allegations; CVX's CFO's Jan 2026 sale was executed under one — scheduled selling, not a discretionary buy/sell signal. |
| **BalanceSheet_Score** | This framework's 0–100 Quality Score sub-score derived from Net Debt/EBITDA. |
| **CAGR** | Compound Annual Growth Rate — the smoothed yearly growth rate that gets you from a start value to an end value over several years. |
| **EBIT / EBITDA** | Earnings Before Interest and Taxes / before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation and Amortization — operating-profit and cash-operating-profit proxies. |
| **EV/EBIT** | Enterprise Value divided by EBIT — a multiple used to compare how expensive companies are relative to their operating profit, independent of capital structure. |
| **FCF Yield** | Free Cash Flow ÷ Market Cap (or Enterprise Value) — how much free cash a company throws off relative to its price; higher is cheaper. |
| **FCF/NI conversion ratio** | Free Cash Flow ÷ Net Income — checks whether reported accounting profit is actually turning into real cash. |
| **FID (Final Investment Decision)** | The formal capital-commitment go-ahead for a large project; Chevron's own FID on the Microsoft data-center power deal is expected only by end-2026 — the deal is signed but not yet a binding capital commitment. |
| **Form 4** | The SEC filing disclosing an insider's (officer/director) change in stock ownership — used here to confirm CVX's CFO transaction was a sale, not a buy. |
| **GAAP** | Generally Accepted Accounting Principles — the standard US accounting rulebook. |
| **Hard disqualifier** | One of three Quality Score conditions that fails a company regardless of its weighted score (see [quality-scoring.md](../../../framework/quality-scoring.md)); none fire for CVX. |
| **Invested Capital** | Debt + Equity − Cash (this framework's net-of-cash convention) — the denominator of ROIC. |
| **M&A** | Mergers & Acquisitions — one company buying or combining with another. |
| **Moat** | A durable competitive advantage that protects a business's profits from competitors. |
| **Net Debt/EBITDA** | Net debt (total debt minus cash) divided by EBITDA — a leverage ratio measuring how many years of operating cash profit it would take to pay off all debt; this framework's primary balance-sheet-risk gate. |
| **NOPAT** | Net Operating Profit After Tax — EBIT × (1 − effective tax rate), the numerator of ROIC. |
| **PPA (Power Purchase Agreement)** | A long-term power-supply contract; Chevron and Microsoft signed a 20-year PPA for a West Texas data center in June 2026. |
| **Quality Score** | This framework's 0.0–100.0 continuous quality grade; a company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02. CVX scores 40.5. |
| **ROIC** | Return on Invested Capital — how efficiently a company turns the capital invested in it (debt + equity) into profit; a core quality signal in this framework. |
| **Rule 0** | This framework's standing instruction to always fetch a live, current price before any valuation work. |
| **Rule 9** | This framework's list of fundamental events that force an immediate re-valuation regardless of schedule: quarterly earnings, a guidance revision, a management change, material M&A, a macro shift, or a >15% stock-price move with no identified cause. |
| **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** | The most recent 12 months of reported financial results. |
| **Turnaround Sub-Gate** | The conditional path (Hybrid Upgrade 4) that lets a company failing some quality criteria still enter as a small (2–3%) position if it passes 5 specific tests (historical ROIC, insider buying, margin of safety, debt level, identifiable moat). |
| **XBRL** | The SEC's structured, machine-readable financial-data tagging format, pulled here via `companyfacts`/`companyconcept` APIs as a yfinance fallback. |
