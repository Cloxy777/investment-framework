## Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **CAGR** | Compound Annual Growth Rate — the smoothed yearly growth rate that gets you from a start value to an end value over several years. |
| **CapEx** | Capital Expenditure — money spent buying or upgrading physical assets (factories, equipment, data centers). |
| **Discontinued operations** | A line item in financial statements that separates out the results of a business unit the company has sold, shut down, or classified as held-for-sale, so it no longer mixes with "continuing operations." When prior-period comparatives are restated to exclude a newly-discontinued unit (e.g. CD Projekt's FY2024 figures restated to exclude GOG.COM following its divestment), year-over-year ratios computed on the restated basis are not directly comparable to figures from the original, non-restated filing. |
| **FCF** | Free Cash Flow — cash a business generates after running and maintaining itself, available to return to shareholders or reinvest. |
| **FCF/NI conversion ratio** | Free Cash Flow ÷ Net Income — checks whether reported accounting profit is actually turning into real cash. A low ratio without a CapEx explanation is a red flag for earnings-quality games. |
| **Hard disqualifier** | One of three Quality Score conditions that fails a company regardless of its weighted Quality Score. Not every hard disqualifier carries a carve-out: the FCF-positivity check has none, while the FCF/NI conversion check can be waived with cited evidence of growth-driven (not maintenance) capex. |
| **IP Box** | A preferential corporate tax regime that taxes profit derived from qualifying intellectual property at a reduced rate — explains CD Projekt's low effective tax rate without implying any accounting irregularity. |
| **Moat** | Warren Buffett's term for a durable competitive advantage (brand, network effect, switching costs, scale) that protects a business's profits from competitors. |
| **Net Debt/EBITDA** | Net debt (total debt minus cash) divided by EBITDA — a leverage ratio measuring how many years of operating cash profit it would take to pay off all debt; this framework's primary balance-sheet-risk gate. A negative figure means net cash, not net debt. |
| **Quality Score** | This framework's 0.0–100.0 continuous score grading the Phase 01 criteria (profitability, margins, growth, balance sheet, moat signal, FCF quality). A company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02 valuation scoring at all. |
| **ROIC** | Return on Invested Capital — how efficiently a company turns the capital invested in it (debt + equity) into profit; a core quality signal in this framework. |
| **Rule 0** | This framework's standing instruction to always fetch a live, current price before any valuation work — never infer price from multiples or stale data, and never treat a Telegram post's claims as a financial input. |
| **Rule 9** | This framework's list of fundamental events that force an immediate re-valuation regardless of schedule: quarterly earnings, a guidance revision, a management change, material M&A, a macro shift, or a >15% stock-price move with no identified cause. |
