## Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **CAGR** | Compound Annual Growth Rate — the smoothed yearly growth rate that gets you from a start value to an end value over several years. |
| **Direct listing** | A way for a company to go public by listing existing shares directly on an exchange, without the traditional IPO process of issuing new shares through underwriters at a set offer price. Results in a shorter trailing public financial/price history than a company that's been listed longer — relevant when a Phase 01 quality-gate condition requires a multi-year lookback (e.g. the Turnaround Sub-Gate's 10-year historical-ROIC check) that a recently-listed company may not have enough history to ever satisfy. |
| **EV/EBIT** | Enterprise Value divided by EBIT — a multiple used to compare how expensive companies are relative to their operating profit, independent of capital structure. |
| **FCF Yield** | Free Cash Flow ÷ Market Cap (or Enterprise Value) — how much free cash a company throws off relative to its price; higher is cheaper. |
| **Hard disqualifier** | One of three Quality Score conditions (FCF/NI conversion <70% for 2+ consecutive years without a documented growth-capex explanation; Net Debt/EBITDA over the Debt Gate threshold; not FCF-positive for 3+ consecutive years) that fails a company regardless of its weighted Quality Score. None fire for PLTR. |
| **Moat** | Warren Buffett's term for a durable competitive advantage (brand, network effect, switching costs, scale) that protects a business's profits from competitors. |
| **Net Debt/EBITDA** | Net debt (total debt minus cash) divided by EBITDA — a leverage ratio measuring how many years of operating cash profit it would take to pay off all debt; this framework's primary balance-sheet-risk gate. A negative figure (as here) means net cash, not net debt. |
| **Net Retention Rate (NRR / Dollar-Based Net Retention)** | The percentage of recurring revenue retained (and expanded, if >100%) from an existing customer cohort over the trailing 12 months, net of downgrades and churn — a proxy for switching costs and pricing power. PLTR reported 150% NRR in Q1 FY2026, cited as Moat Signal (Brand premium) evidence in this framework's 2026-07-10 addendum. |
| **NGC2 (Next Generation Command and Control)** | The US Army's highest-priority modernization program to unify battlefield command-and-control data onto a single shared architecture. The Army built NGC2's foundational data layer on Palantir Foundry (with Anduril's Lattice providing the edge-to-cloud data mesh) — cited as confirmation of the triggering post's "US military data layer" claim in this framework's 2026-07-10 PLTR addendum, alongside the pre-existing (July 2025) $10B, 10-year Army Enterprise Agreement. |
| **Ontology (Palantir)** | Palantir's proprietary framework for modeling an organization's data, business logic, and decision-making actions into one connected representation inside Foundry/AIP — sometimes described as a "digital twin" of the organization. Once an organization's workflows, data mappings, security rules, and decisions are built inside it, migrating to a competing platform means rebuilding that semantic modeling from scratch — cited as Switching Costs Moat Signal evidence in this framework's 2026-07-10 PLTR addendum. |
| **Quality Score** | This framework's 0.0–100.0 continuous score (0.0 = lowest quality, 100.0 = highest) grading profitability, margins, growth, balance sheet, moat signal, and FCF quality. A company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02 valuation scoring at all. PLTR scores 88.0 on its first-ever computation (2026-07-10) — clears the gate despite failing Phase 01 on price-denominated cheapness filters. |
| **ROIC** | Return on Invested Capital — how efficiently a company turns the capital invested in it (debt + equity) into profit; a core quality signal in this framework. |
| **Rule 0** | This framework's non-negotiable rule to always fetch live prices and financial data fresh, never infer or estimate them — including never treating a Telegram post's claims as a financial input. |
| **Rule 9** | This framework's list of fundamental events that force an immediate re-valuation regardless of schedule: quarterly earnings, a guidance revision, a management change, material M&A, a macro shift, or a >15% stock-price move with no identified cause. |
| **TCV (Total Contract Value)** | The full dollar value of a signed customer contract over its entire term (distinct from ACV, which annualizes it). PLTR's Q1 FY2026 TCV closed was $2.41B, +61% YoY — cited as growth-momentum evidence in this framework's 2026-07-10 addendum. |
| **Turnaround Sub-Gate** | The conditional path (Hybrid Upgrade 4) that lets a company failing some quality criteria still enter as a small (2–3%) position if it passes 5 specific tests (historical ROIC, insider buying, margin of safety, debt level, identifiable moat). |
