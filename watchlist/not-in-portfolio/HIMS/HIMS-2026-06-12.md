## Glossary

See [framework/glossary.md](../../../framework/glossary.md) for the standing definitions file. Terms used in this addendum:

- **8-K** — a US company's "current report" filed with the SEC disclosing a material event between regular filings; earnings press releases are typically furnished as an exhibit to one.
- **Adjusted EBITDA** — a company's own non-GAAP variant of EBITDA that strips out items management deems non-recurring (e.g. restructuring charges) — not directly comparable to a GAAP-derived figure.
- **BalanceSheet_Score** — this framework's 0–100 Quality Score sub-score derived from Net Debt/EBITDA.
- **CAGR** — Compound Annual Growth Rate.
- **DTC (Direct-to-Consumer)** — a business model that sells directly to end customers (here, via telehealth) rather than through intermediaries like traditional brick-and-mortar clinics or pharmacies.
- **FCF/NI conversion ratio** — Free Cash Flow ÷ Net Income, an earnings-quality check.
- **GAAP** — Generally Accepted Accounting Principles.
- **Gross Margin** — Gross Profit ÷ Revenue.
- **Hard disqualifier** — one of three Quality Score conditions that fails a company regardless of its weighted score; none fire for HIMS in this addendum.
- **Moat** — a durable competitive advantage.
- **NI (Net Income)** — accounting profit after all expenses, interest, and taxes.
- **PCAC (Pharmacy Compounding Advisory Committee)** — an FDA advisory committee that evaluates substances (here, peptides) for inclusion on the list of drugs compounding pharmacies may legally prepare; convening 23–24 July 2026 to review 7 peptides not including semaglutide.
- **Quality Score** — this framework's 0–100.0 continuous quality grade; a company must score 80.0+ to proceed to Phase 02. HIMS scores 49.1, its first-ever score under this engine.
- **ROIC** — Return on Invested Capital.
- **Rule 0** — always fetch a live price before any valuation work.
- **Rule 9** — the framework's list of events that force an immediate re-valuation: earnings, guidance revision, management change, M&A, macro shift, or a >15% unexplained price move.
- **TTM (Trailing Twelve Months)** — the most recent 12 months of reported results.
